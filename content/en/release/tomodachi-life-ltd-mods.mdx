## Preventing Housebound Miis in the LTD Demo

If you are currently playing the Tomodachi Life: LTD demo, you may notice that after reaching certain milestones, your Miis stop visiting the fountain or the park and instead stay inside their apartments. This "housebound" status effectively ends the gameplay loop. To keep your island thriving for as long as possible, you must avoid specific progression triggers that the game uses to signal the "end" of the demo experience.

### 1. Avoid Unlocking the Clothing Store
The most critical trigger for ending the interactive portion of the LTD demo is the clothing store. In the standard game, unlocking shops is a priority, but in the LTD demo, this action flags the save file as "completed."

*   **The Trigger:** When your first Mii develops a yellow thought bubble, they will often ask for new clothes.
*   **The Consequence:** Fulfilling this request unlocks the clothing store, which immediately restricts your Miis to their homes.
*   **The Solution:** Ignore the request for clothes. You can continue to feed your Miis and interact with them in other ways, but providing them with a wardrobe change will effectively "lobotomize" their social AI in the demo version.

### 2. Managing Mii Dreams
Dreams are a staple of the series, but in the LTD modded demo, they act as a secondary trigger for the "housebound" state.

*   **The Behavior:** Usually, the second Mii added to the island will be the one to start dreaming. 
*   **The Risk:** Clicking on a dreaming Mii often leads to a scripted sequence that concludes the demo's free-play mode.
*   **The Workaround:** While it can be annoying to see a Mii constantly returning to the fountain to dream, it is better to physically drag them away or ignore the dream bubble entirely. This keeps the Mii active in the world rather than locked behind a door.

### 3. Limiting Island Population
While it is tempting to fill your island with friends and celebrities immediately, the LTD demo handles population growth as a progression metric.

> **Warning:** Adding a third Mii is the direct precursor to the clothing store prompt. For the longest possible playtime in the LTD demo, it is recommended to stick with a two-Mii island.

## Advanced Installation of Tomodachi Life LTD Mods

For those who have moved beyond the demo and are looking to apply **tomodachi life ltd mods** to the full retail version of the game, the process involves using "LayerFS." This method allows the 3DS (or Citra) to replace original game files with modded ones on the fly without permanently altering the base game.

### Prerequisites for Modding
Before attempting to install any modifications, ensure you have the following:
1.  A Nintendo 3DS with Luma3DS custom firmware installed.
2.  The original Tomodachi Life game (digital or physical).
3.  A computer with an SD card reader.
4.  The LTD mod files (usually provided as a `romfs` folder).

### Step-by-Step LayerFS Setup
1.  **Locate Title ID:** Every game has a unique Title ID. For Tomodachi Life (USA), it is `000400000008C300`. For Europe, it is `000400000008C400`.
2.  **Prepare SD Card:** Navigate to the `luma/titles/` directory on your SD card.
3.  **Create Folder:** Create a new folder named after your game's Title ID.
4.  **Transfer Files:** Place the modded `romfs` folder inside the Title ID folder.
5.  **Enable Patching:** Hold the 'Select' button while booting your 3DS to enter the Luma3DS configuration menu. Ensure "Enable game patching" is checked.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use Tomodachi Life LTD mods on a non-homebrewed 3DS?**

A: No, you must have custom firmware (CFW) like Luma3DS installed to use LayerFS, which is the primary method for loading **tomodachi life ltd mods**.

**Q: How do I fix Miis that are already stuck in their houses in the LTD demo?**

A: Unfortunately, once the "housebound" state is triggered in the demo, there is no in-game way to reverse it. You will need to delete your save data or create a new user account on your 3DS to start a fresh island on a different "island slot."

**Q: Are Tomodachi Life LTD mods compatible with the Citra emulator?**

A: Yes, Citra supports modding via the "Load Manual Texture" and "LayeredFS" features. Right-click the game in your Citra library and select "Open Mods Location" to place your mod files.

**Q: Is the "Living the Dream" mod the same as the "Tomodachi Life: Deluxe" mod?**

A: They are separate projects. While both aim to improve the game, LTD focuses more on social mechanics and modernizing the "vibe" of the island, whereas Deluxe often focuses on massive item additions. Always check the specific documentation for each mod to avoid file conflicts.
