## Understanding the Mii Apartments Capacity

Before diving into the suburban housing, it is vital to understand the primary residence for all islanders: the Mii Apartments. Every Mii you create or import via QR Code will initially reside in the apartment complex located at the center of the island. 

The apartment building is not static; it grows as your population increases. Initially, the building is small, but it can expand multiple times to accommodate more residents. The final capacity of the Mii Apartments is 100 Miis. This is the hard cap for any single island save file.

Once you hit the 100-Mii limit, you cannot add any more residents unless you delete an existing Mii or send a child off to travel the world via StreetPass. This 100-person limit is the foundation for calculating **how many people can live in a house tomodachi life** across the entire island ecosystem.

## How Many People Can Live in a House Tomodachi Life?

When Miis get married, they unlock a new area on the island called "Mii Homes." This is a suburban neighborhood where married couples spend most of their time. However, the game handles "living" in a unique way. Even when a Mii is married and has a house, they technically still "own" their apartment in the main complex. This allows them to interact with single Miis and participate in apartment-specific events.

In terms of the physical house in the Mii Homes section, the occupancy is strictly regulated by the game's family mechanics. 

### The Married Couple
Every house is occupied by exactly **two adults**: the married couple. You cannot have roommates, polyamorous relationships, or multi-generational households (such as grandparents living with children) in a single house. The house is a private space for the two Miis who have successfully navigated the "Confession" and "Proposal" stages of their relationship.

### The Arrival of Babies
If you have the "Babies" setting turned on in the Town Hall, married couples will eventually ask if they should have a child. When a baby is born, the occupancy of the house temporarily increases. For the duration of the baby's infancy and childhood, the house will contain **three people**: the two parents and the child.

> **💡 Tip:** You can only have one baby per household at a time. A couple will not ask for another child until the current one has fully grown up and moved out.

| Resident Type | Number of People | Duration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Married Couple** | 2 | Permanent (while married) |
| **Infant/Child** | 1 | Until adulthood (approx. 5-7 days) |
| **Maximum Occupancy** | **3** | Temporary during child-rearing |

While the answer to **how many people can live in a house tomodachi life** is technically three during the baby phase, the permanent resident count is always two. Once the child grows up, you are given the choice to either move them into their own unit in the Mii Apartments or let them become a traveler. In either scenario, they vacate the family home, returning the occupancy to two.

## Unlocking and Managing Mii Homes

To even begin worrying about house occupancy, you must first unlock the Mii Homes area. This requires a specific set of circumstances to occur within your game.

1.  **Relationship Building:** Two Miis of opposite genders must become "Sweethearts."
2.  **The Proposal:** One Mii must eventually ask to propose. You must successfully complete the proposal mini-game.
3.  **The Wedding:** After the proposal, the wedding occurs immediately, and the Mii Homes area is automatically placed on your island map.

There is no limit to the number of houses that can exist in the Mii Homes area, other than the overall population cap. Since there are 100 Miis maximum, you could theoretically have up to **50 married couples**, resulting in 50 houses in the suburban area.

## Managing Your Island Population in 2026

With a maximum of 100 residents and 50 potential houses, population management is a balancing act. If you fill your island with 100 Miis too quickly, you will not have room for any babies to grow up and move into the apartments.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If your Mii Apartments are full (100/100), any child born on the island *must* become a traveler. They cannot stay on the island as a permanent resident because there is no vacant apartment for them.

To maintain a healthy island, many expert players recommend keeping your population around **80 to 85 Miis**. This leaves "buffer" rooms in the apartment complex for:
1.  **New Babies:** Allowing them to grow up and stay on the island.
2.  **Special Guests:** Importing Miis from friends or celebrities via QR codes.
3.  **Future Updates:** Keeping space open for any potential Miis you might want to create later.

## The Role of the Town Hall

The Town Hall is the administrative hub where you can track the statistics of your island. If you are unsure about **how many people can live in a house tomodachi life** or how many residents you currently have, the Town Hall provides a clear breakdown.

*   **Mii List:** Shows every resident and their current address (Apartment number).
*   **Album:** View photos of families and their homes.
*   **Settings:** Toggle the ability for babies to be born.

If you find that your island is becoming too crowded, the Town Hall is also where you can "delete" a Mii. When a Mii is deleted, they disappear from the island, and if they were married, their spouse becomes single again and the house in the Mii Homes area is removed.

## FAQ

**Q: Can two Miis live in the same apartment?**

A: No. Each Mii, regardless of age or relationship status, is assigned their own individual apartment. Even married Miis keep their original apartments, though they spend most of their time together in the Mii Homes section.

**Q: What is the maximum number of people that can be in one house at the same time?**

A: The maximum number of people you will see in a house simultaneously is three: the two parents and one baby. Occasionally, friends may visit, but they do not "live" there and will leave after a short period.

**Q: How many houses can I have on my island?**

A: There is no specific numerical cap on the number of houses, but since each house requires a married couple and the total population is capped at 100, the functional limit is 50 houses.

**Q: Can children stay in the house forever?**

A: No. In Tomodachi Life, children grow up very quickly (usually within a week). Once they reach adulthood, they must either move into their own apartment or depart the island to travel via StreetPass. They cannot remain in their parents' house indefinitely.

**Q: Does the size of the house change?**

A: While the interior of the house can be decorated with different "interiors" (available via the Import Wear or special events), the physical size and the number of people who can live in a house in Tomodachi Life remains the same regardless of the couple's wealth or status.
