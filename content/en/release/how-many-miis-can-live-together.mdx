## Official Population Limits in 2026

Nintendo has officially confirmed the population capacity for the latest installment in the franchise. This number has been a point of significant discussion among veteran players, especially those transitioning from the older Nintendo 3DS version of the game.

While the number 70 might seem specific, it is a hard cap set by the developers to ensure the game runs smoothly on the current hardware. This limit includes all Miis you create or import, regardless of their age or relationship status on the island.

| Feature | Tomodachi Life (3DS) | Tomodachi Life: Living The Dream (Switch, 2026) |

## Why the Limit Changed

The reduction from 100 Miis on the 3DS to 70 on the Nintendo Switch has surprised some fans. However, the reasoning lies in the ambitious gameplay upgrades introduced in the 2026 edition. In the original game, Miis were largely confined to their apartment windows or specific static locations. In *Living The Dream*, the Miis are much more active.

They now appear in the overworld map frequently, wandering through parks, visiting shops, and interacting with one another in real-time. Processing the AI logic and unique animations for 70 Miis simultaneously in a 3D environment is significantly more demanding than the previous 2D-style apartment grid. Technical experts suggest that maintaining a stable frame rate while these characters roam freely required a slightly lower population cap.

## Official Workarounds for More Miis

If you find that 70 slots are not enough for your creative vision, there is a very simple workaround. Unlike *Animal Crossing: New Horizons*, which restricted players to one island per console, *Tomodachi Life: Living The Dream* allows for multiple save files through the use of Nintendo Switch system profiles.

By creating additional user profiles on your console, you can start entirely new islands. This effectively multiplies **how many miis can live together** across your device.

| Strategy | Benefit | Drawback |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Themed Islands** | Dedicate one island to family and another to celebrities. | Islands cannot interact directly with each other. |
| **Profile Swapping** | Allows for up to 8 different islands per console. | Requires switching users and managing separate progress. |
| **Mii QR Codes** | Easily move your favorite Miis between your different islands. | Relationships and levels do not transfer between saves. |

## Managing Your Island Population

With a limit of 70, you must be more selective about who joins your community. Managing **how many miis can live together** requires a bit of strategy, especially if you want to see the full range of social interactions, such as marriages and children.

1.  **Prioritize Variety:** Ensure you have a mix of personalities (Easygoing, Outgoing, Independent, etc.) to trigger more diverse events.
2.  **Monitor Relationships:** If your island is full, babies born to married couples will not be able to grow up and move into their own apartments unless you delete a Mii first.
3.  **Use the Town Hall:** Regularly check your Mii list at the Town Hall to see who is contributing the least to your island's social life.
4.  **Export Regularly:** If you are attached to a Mii but need to make room, save their QR code so you can bring them back later or move them to a secondary island.

> **Tip:** Keep at least 2-3 slots open if you plan on letting your Miis get married and have children, as this prevents the "Full Island" notification from blocking major life events.

The decision regarding **how many miis can live together** isn't just about storage space; it's about the "Living" aspect of the game. Each Mii in the 2026 version has:
*   Unique pathfinding for overworld navigation.
*   Dynamic relationship tracking with 69 other individuals.

When you multiply these factors by 70, the CPU load becomes substantial. By capping the population, Nintendo ensures that the island remains vibrant without suffering from lag or crashes, which would ruin the immersive experience of watching your Miis live their daily lives.

## FAQ

**Q: Exactly how many miis can live together in Tomodachi Life: Living The Dream?**

A: In the official 2026 release for the Nintendo Switch, a maximum of 70 Miis can live together on a single island. This is the confirmed limit per save file.

**Q: Can I increase the 70-Mii limit with an SD card?**

A: No, the limit is a software restriction tied to game performance and AI processing, not physical storage space. Adding a larger SD card will not increase the population cap.

**Q: What happens if a baby is born when I have 70 Miis?**

A: If your island is at the maximum capacity of 70, the baby can still be born and live with its parents. However, once the baby reaches the age to move out, you will be forced to either send them to travel via StreetPass/SpotPass or delete another Mii to make room for them in the apartments.

**Q: Is there any difference in the limit between the physical and digital versions?**

A: No, both the physical cartridge and the digital eShop version of *Tomodachi Life: Living The Dream* have the exact same 70-Mii population limit.
