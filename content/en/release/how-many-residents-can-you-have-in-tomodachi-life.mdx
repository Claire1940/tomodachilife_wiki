## The Maximum Resident Capacity

In Tomodachi Life, the primary hub for your Miis is the Mii Apartments. When you first start your island in 2026, the building is relatively small, but it has the potential to grow into a massive skyscraper. The absolute maximum number of residents you can have in Tomodachi Life is **100 Miis**. 

This limit is fixed and applies to the total number of Miis living in the apartment complex at any given time. While 100 might seem like a large number, the island fills up quickly once Miis start getting married and having children. It is important to note that while babies born on the island do not immediately take up a permanent slot in the 100-resident count, they will require a room if they grow up and decide to stay on the island rather than becoming travelers.

## Expanding the Mii Apartments

The apartment building does not stay at its starting capacity for long. The game automatically expands the building as you add more Miis. This progression is seamless and occurs once you reach specific population thresholds. You do not need to spend play coins or in-game currency to trigger these upgrades; you simply need to keep adding residents via the Mii Maker or the Town Hall.

Once you hit the final expansion at 56 residents, the building reaches its maximum physical height. From that point on, you can continue to add Miis until you reach the hard cap of 100. If you attempt to add a 101st Mii, the game will prompt you to delete an existing resident to make room.

> **💡 Tip:** To quickly fill your island, use the QR Code scanner in the Town Hall to import Miis created by other players online. This is the fastest way to test the limits of your apartment complex.

## Managing a Full Island

When you reach the limit of **how many residents can you have in Tomodachi Life**, management becomes a game of "one in, one out." To maintain a healthy island ecosystem in 2026, you may occasionally need to remove residents who aren't contributing to the drama or fun of your save file.

### How to Remove Residents
1. Go to the **Town Hall**.
2. Select the **Mii Settings** option.
3. Choose the Mii you wish to move out.
4. Select **Delete** (Note: This is permanent and removes all their progress/items).

### The Role of Travelers
Travelers are Miis who are the children of your residents but have chosen to travel the world via StreetPass rather than living in the apartments. Travelers do not count toward your 100-resident limit. They live in a separate "camp" area when visiting other islands, allowing you to effectively have a "legacy" of Miis that exceeds the hard cap of the apartment building.

## Resident Interactions and Building Life

Having 100 residents isn't just about the number; it’s about the interactions. The more Miis you have, the higher the frequency of "bubbles" appearing over the apartment windows. These bubbles signify:
*   **Friendship Requests:** Miis wanting to meet someone new.
*   **Problems:** Miis needing food, clothes, or medicine.
*   **Games:** Miis wanting to play minigames like "Matchmaker" or "Mii Pixel."
*   **Love Interests:** Miis developing crushes or asking for relationship advice.

A full building of 100 residents can be overwhelming to manage, as the needs of the Miis will trigger constantly. Most veteran players suggest keeping the population between 80 and 90 to allow room for island-born children to grow up and move in.

## FAQ

**Q: How many residents can you have in Tomodachi Life at the very maximum?**

A: The hard limit for the Mii Apartments is 100 residents. Once you reach this number, you cannot add any more Miis unless you delete an existing one or a resident's child decides to become a traveler.

**Q: Do babies count toward the 100-resident limit?**

A: While they are infants living with their parents in the Mii Homes, they do not count toward the apartment limit. However, once they grow up, if you choose for them to "Stay on the Island," they will move into their own apartment and take up one of the 100 slots.

**Q: Does the apartment building ever stop growing?**

A: The building stops expanding physically once you reach the "Extra Large" stage, which happens when you have 56 or more residents. It will then accommodate up to 100 residents without further external changes.

**Q: Can I have more than 100 Miis if I use StreetPass?**

A: You can only have 100 permanent residents. However, you can host several "Travelers" at the Campground via StreetPass. These travelers are temporary visitors and do not take up permanent residence in your apartment building.
