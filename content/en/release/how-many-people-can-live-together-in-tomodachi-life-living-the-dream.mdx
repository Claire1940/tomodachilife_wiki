However, the "living together" aspect is slightly more nuanced than just a single number. ## Managing Couples and Families

A unique aspect of **how many people can live together in tomodachi life living the dream** involves married couples. When two Miis get married, they do not technically "move in" to a single apartment in the main building. Instead, they keep their individual apartments but also move into a **Mii House** in the residential area.

*   **Apartment Usage:** Even if a Mii is married and spends most of their time in a house, they still occupy one of the 100 slots in the apartment building.
*   **Children:** When a couple has a baby, the child lives in the house with the parents. Once the child grows up, they can either move into their own apartment (if you have space within your 100-Mii limit) or become a traveler.
*   **Travelers:** Travelers do not count toward your 100-Mii apartment limit. They stay at the Campground temporarily before moving to another island via StreetPass or local wireless.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If your apartment building is at 100/100 capacity, a grown-up child *cannot* move into the apartments. They must become a traveler, or you must delete an existing resident to make room.

## Strategies for a Full Island

Reaching the maximum population of 100 can make the game feel chaotic. Here is how to manage a full house effectively in 2026:

1.  **Use the Mii Library:** Keep a backup of your favorite Miis in the Mii Maker so you can swap residents in and out without losing their physical designs.
2.  **Monitor Relationships:** Use the "Relationship" office in the Town Hall to see who is getting along. If your island is at max capacity and two Miis are constantly fighting, it might be time to let one move out.
3.  **Diversify Personalities:** Refer back to the personality chart. An island with 100 "Leader" types will be much more volatile than one with a healthy mix of "Easygoing" and "Reserved" types.
4.  **Check the News:** The [Official Nintendo Support site](https://en-americas-support.nintendo.com/) often provides updates on how local wireless features work for those looking to send their 101st Mii out as a traveler.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I increase the limit beyond 100 Miis in the Living the Dream version?**

A: No, the engine limit for the apartment building is strictly 100 Miis. This is a technical constraint of the original software architecture that persists even in updated versions or mods.

**Q: Do babies count toward the 100-person limit?**

A: Babies do not count toward the limit while they are infants living at home with their parents. However, once they grow up, they will only stay on the island as permanent residents if there is an empty slot in the 100-room apartment building.

**Q: What happens if I try to add a 101st person?**

A: The game will inform you that the apartments are full. To add more people, you must go to the Town Hall and select an existing Mii to "Move Out," which permanently deletes them from your island.

**Q: How many people can live together in tomodachi life living the dream if I include travelers?**

A: While only 100 people can have permanent apartments, you can have multiple travelers staying at your Campground simultaneously. These travelers are transient and will eventually leave for other islands, so they provide a way to see new faces without using up your 100 permanent slots.
