This single, massive building could be expanded multiple times, eventually reaching a maximum height that housed exactly **100 Miis**.

However, the 2026 landscape has changed with the "Living the Dream" version. In this edition, the focus has shifted from high-density apartment living to individual houses spread across an open world. Because each resident now requires a physical building footprint on the island map, the total capacity has been adjusted to **70 Miis**. While this is a lower raw number, the depth of interaction for each resident is significantly higher.

## Housing Dynamics and Population Growth

In the modern 2026 version of the game, managing **how many residents in tomodachi life** you have is no longer just about filling slots in a menu. Each resident now has a tangible impact on the island's layout. When you add a new Mii, you must consider where their house will sit. 

The "Mii House" system is the most significant change. Unlike the 3DS version, where married couples lived in a house but maintained their individual apartment rooms, the new system allows up to eight Miis to move in together as their friendships or romantic relationships develop. This creates a much more dynamic social environment.

## Population Management and "Warm Fuzzies"

The goal of having a high number of residents is not just for show; it is to generate "Warm Fuzzies." This is the primary resource used to upgrade your island in the 2026 version. Every time you feed a resident a food they love at **Fresh Kingdom** or help them solve a problem, they generate these points.

The **Wishing Fountain** acts as the collection point for this energy. You can spend Warm Fuzzies on:
1.  **Island Upgrades:** Expanding the physical size of the island to fit more houses.
2.  **Mii Quirks:** Unique animations or speech patterns for specific residents.
3.  **New Goods:** Unlocking rare items in the shops that weren't previously available.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If you ignore your residents' needs for too long, their happiness levels will drop, and they will stop generating Warm Fuzzies, effectively stalling your island's progression.

## Customizing Your Residents' Lives

With the introduction of the **Pallet House**, the residents you choose to bring to your island are more customizable than ever. You are no longer limited to the items provided by the game's default shops. You can design custom clothes, foods, and even house interiors for each of your 70 or 100 residents.

The **TNC Reno** shop allows you to change the exterior design of every Mii's home. In the 3DS version, interiors were essentially just static wallpapers for a menu screen. In 2026, the interior you design is a 3D space you can actually visit and explore with the camera. This makes the resident's living space feel like a true reflection of their personality.

## FAQ

**Q: How many residents in Tomodachi Life can live in one house?**

A: In the 2026 "Living the Dream" version, up to eight Miis can share a single house as their relationships grow. In the original 3DS version, Miis lived in individual apartment rooms, though married couples shared a separate home for daytime visits.

**Q: Is there a way to increase the 100-Mii limit on 3DS?**

A: No, the 100-Mii limit is a hard cap based on the hardware's memory. Once you reach 100 residents, you must delete an existing Mii to add a new one.

**Q: Does the resident count include babies born on the island?**

A: Yes. Once a baby grows up and becomes a permanent resident, they take up one of the available slots (70 or 100, depending on your version). If your island is full, the child must be sent off via StreetPass/Local Wireless to travel the world instead of staying as a resident.

**Q: Can I have multiple copies of the same resident?**

A: Technically, yes. You can scan the same QR code multiple times to add identical Miis, but they will be treated as entirely separate individuals with their own unique food preferences and relationship histories.
