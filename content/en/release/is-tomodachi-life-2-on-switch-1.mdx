## Island Progression and the Leveling System

One of the most requested features for a sequel was a more structured sense of progression. *Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream* introduces **Island Levels**. ### Unlockable Rewards by Level

1. **Level 2:** Unlocks basic goods like soccer balls and bubble blowers.
2. **Level 5:** Grants access to the clothing store's premium "snazzy" collection.
3. **Level 10:** Unlocks the ability to create custom phrases and island lingo.
4. **Level 20:** Provides the "Island Builder" tool for deeper environmental customization.

As you interact with your residents, you will collect "mysterious orbs." These orbs appear when Miis reach high levels of happiness and serve as a currency for purchasing rare upgrades or unlocking unique events on the island.

While the original game relied heavily on RNG (random number generation) for relationships, the Switch version allows players to have a "helping hand." This doesn't guarantee success, but it speeds up the process of them becoming acquaintances or even sweethearts.

Relationships are now categorized with more nuance. Miis can be:
*   **Acquaintances:** Just starting to know each other.
*   **Like-Minded Friends:** Developing a strong bond through shared activities.
*   **Sweethearts:** Exploring a romantic connection.
*   **Rivals:** Occasionally bumping heads over shared interests or friends.

> **Warning:** Be careful when trying to force interactions. If a Mii is "sulking" or having a bad day, pushing them into a social situation might lead to a fight, which can negatively impact the island's overall happiness levels.
