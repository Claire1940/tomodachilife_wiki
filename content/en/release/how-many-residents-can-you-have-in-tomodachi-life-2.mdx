## Maximum Resident Capacity and Apartment Upgrades

In the 2026 iteration of the franchise, Nintendo has significantly expanded the technical capabilities of the island. While the original 3DS title capped your population at 100 Miis, the hardware of the current generation allows for a more robust community. In *Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream*, the base apartment building starts small but grows as you add more residents and complete specific island milestones.

The maximum number of residents you can currently house in the main apartment complex is **150 Miis**. This is a 50% increase over the previous game, allowing for much more diverse social interactions and a higher chance of unique personality pairings. However, you do not start with 150 slots available immediately. You must trigger expansion events by filling your current rooms.

### Apartment Expansion Stages

| Building Stage | Resident Capacity | Unlock Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Initial Building** | 20 Rooms | Start a new game |
| **First Expansion** | 50 Rooms | Reach 15 residents |
| **Second Expansion** | 75 Rooms | Reach 40 residents |
| **Third Expansion** | 100 Rooms | Reach 60 residents |
| **Luxury Wing** | 150 Rooms | Reach 90 residents + 5 Married Couples |

> **💡 Tip:** To quickly trigger expansions, focus on importing "Simple Miis" from your system settings or Miitopia data to fill slots, even if you plan to delete or edit them later.

As of 2026, you can use the "Access Key" system in *Miitopia* (even the free demo) to download complex Mii designs from the community-driven "Miipedia" database. Once these characters are saved to your Nintendo Switch system, you can import them directly into your island.

## Managing a Large Population

Once you understand **how many residents can you have in tomodachi life 2**, the next challenge is managing them. A 150-resident island is significantly more chaotic than a 100-resident one. The frequency of "Problem" bubbles (the black icons over windows) increases, and the social feed moves much faster.

### The Impact of Full Capacity
1.  **Relationship Dynamics**: With more residents, the "Relationship" tab becomes a massive web. You are more likely to see "Love Triangles" and "Best Friend" disputes.
2.  **Island Facilities**: Buildings like the Fountain, Cafe, and Park will be more crowded. This increases the chance of seeing Miis interacting in groups of four or five, rather than just pairs.
3.  **Performance**: On the standard Nintendo Switch, a full 150-resident island may see slightly longer loading times when entering the Apartment view compared to a fresh save.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful about deleting residents to make room for new ones. Deleting a Mii removes all their social history, which can cause "Sadness" status for their remaining friends and family members on the island.

## Expanding Beyond the Apartments: Travelers and Babies

It is important to note that the 150-resident cap specifically refers to the permanent residents living in the apartment complex. There are "temporary" residents that do not count against this specific total:

*   **Island Babies**: When a married couple has a child, the baby lives with the parents in the Mii Homes area. They only take up an apartment slot if you choose to make them a permanent resident once they grow up.
*   **Travelers**: Miis visiting via StreetPass or local wireless stay at the Campground. They do not occupy apartment rooms, meaning you can technically have more than 150 Miis on your island at any given time if you have a high volume of visitors.

For more information on official updates and potential DLC that might further increase the population cap, visit the [official Nintendo website](https://www.nintendo.com).

## Social Mechanics at High Resident Counts

In *Tomodachi Life 2*, the "Living the Dream" engine handles social interactions differently when you approach the 150-Mii limit. The game prioritizes "Active Relationships." This means the Miis you interact with most frequently are more likely to appear in the front-facing windows of the apartment building.

If you have a full house, you may find it difficult to keep everyone happy. It is recommended to use the "Group Gift" feature, unlocked after reaching 100 residents, which allows you to give the same food or clothing item to an entire floor of the apartment at once. This drastically reduces the micro-management required for a maximum-capacity island.

## FAQ

**Q: Exactly how many residents can you have in tomodachi life 2?**

A: In the 2026 release *Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream*, you can have a maximum of 150 permanent residents in your apartment complex. This is an increase from the 100-resident limit found in the original 3DS game.

**Q: Does the resident limit include babies?**

A: No, babies live in the "Mii Homes" section with their parents. They only count toward the 150-resident limit if they grow up and you choose to give them their own apartment instead of sending them off to travel.

**Q: Can I increase the resident limit past 150?**

A: Currently, 150 is the hard cap for the apartment building. While there are rumors of a "Metropolis DLC" later in 2026 that may add a second apartment tower, Nintendo has not officially confirmed this.

**Q: Do travelers from other islands take up a room?**

A: No, travelers stay at the Campground facility. They are temporary visitors and do not count against your 150-resident apartment capacity.
