## The Distinction Between Apartments and Mii Homes

In the world of Tomodachi Life, "housing" is split into two distinct categories: the Mii Apartments and Mii Homes. The Mii Apartments serve as the primary residence for all Miis you create or import to the island. Every single Mii has their own dedicated room in the apartment building, which they decorate and inhabit as individuals.

Mii Homes, on the other hand, are a separate residential area that unlocks only after two Miis get married. When a couple ties the knot, they receive a private house in the "Mii Homes" section of the map. However, it is important to note that even when a Mii is married and has a house, they still technically maintain their room in the apartment building. They will split their time between the two locations, though they will spend the majority of their nights at the house with their spouse.

## How Many People Can Live in One House in Tomodachi Life?

When looking specifically at the private residences found in the Mii Homes area, the answer to **how many people can live in one house in tomodachi life** is typically two adults. Once a male and female Mii reach a high enough relationship level and one proposes to the other, they move into a house together. This house is a shared space where they interact, eat, and sleep.

However, this number can temporarily increase. If the couple decides to have a child, the baby will live in the house with the parents. During the infancy and toddler stages, the baby is a permanent fixture in the home. Once the child grows up, they either move into their own apartment (if there is space) or depart the island to become a traveler. Therefore, at its peak, a single house can hold a family of three (two parents and one child) simultaneously.

> **💡 Tip:** To encourage a couple to have a baby, ensure their relationship status remains at "Great" or "Happily Married" by giving them gifts and resolving any minor tiffs they may have.

## Managing the Apartment Building Capacity

While the private houses are for families, the Mii Apartments are the heart of your island's population. The apartment building starts small but expands as you add more Miis. The final expansion allows for a maximum of 100 Miis to live on your island. 

It is vital to manage this space effectively. If you reach the 100-Mii limit, you will not be able to add new Miis or allow children born on the island to move into their own rooms. In such cases, children must be sent off as travelers via StreetPass (or local wireless in 2026) to explore other players' islands.

## Family Dynamics and Moving Out

The "house" mechanic is deeply tied to the lifecycle of a Mii family. When a baby is born, the gameplay shifts slightly to include childcare minigames. During this period, the parents are almost always found at the house rather than the apartments. 

Once the child reaches the "Grown Up" stage, you are presented with a choice. If you choose to have the child stay on the island, they will move out of the Mii Home and into a vacant room in the Mii Apartments. At this point, the occupancy of the Mii Home reverts to the original two parents. If you have no vacant rooms in the apartments, the child *must* become a traveler, or you must delete an existing resident to make room.

## The "Housebound" Issue: Demo vs. Full Game

As discussed in various community guides, players using the *Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream* demo may experience a different version of housing. In the demo, Miis can sometimes become "housebound" or locked in their rooms if certain conditions are met, such as unlocking the clothing store prematurely. In the full version of the game, Miis are never truly trapped; they have the freedom to visit the fountain, the beach, or other islanders' apartments.

If you are playing the full retail version in 2026, you don't need to worry about the "lobotomized advertisement" state mentioned in demo-specific tutorials. Your Miis will actively move between their apartments and the Mii Homes based on their social needs and relationship status.

> **⚠️ Warning:** In the demo version, adding a third Mii or opening the clothing store can trigger a "locked" state where Miis only advertise the full game. Avoid these triggers if you wish to keep playing the demo indefinitely.

## Strategies for Optimizing Island Housing

To make the most of your island, consider the following strategies for managing your residents:

*   **Gender Balance:** Maintain a roughly equal number of male and female Miis to maximize the potential for marriages and Mii Homes.
*   **Quality over Quantity:** If you are nearing the 100-Mii limit, consider deleting Miis that you aren't attached to. This leaves "house" space for the children of your favorite couples.
*   **Check the Mii Homes Area:** Even if a Mii isn't in their apartment, they might be at their house. Always check both locations if you are looking for a specific resident to give them a gift or solve a problem.

## FAQ

**Q: How many people can live in one house in Tomodachi Life if they aren't married?**

A: In Tomodachi Life, only married couples can live together in a house. Single Miis, even those who are dating or best friends, must live in their own separate rooms in the apartment building. There is no "roommate" mechanic for unmarried Miis.

**Q: Can a house hold more than one baby at a time?**

A: No, a couple will only have one baby at a time. Once that child has grown up and moved out (either to the apartments or to travel the world), the couple may eventually decide to have another baby, but the house occupancy for children is limited to one at a time.

**Q: What happens to the house if a couple gets divorced?**

A: If a couple gets divorced, the Mii Home is immediately removed from the map. Both Miis will move back to their respective rooms in the Mii Apartments full-time. Any items or memories associated specifically with the house are lost, though the Miis retain their individual levels and inventories.

**Q: Can I customize the exterior of the houses in the Mii Homes area?**

A: While you can customize the interior of a Mii's apartment using various "Interiors" purchased from the shop, the exterior and interior of the Mii Homes are generated automatically and cannot be manually decorated by the player. The house's appearance is generally standard across all married couples on the island.
