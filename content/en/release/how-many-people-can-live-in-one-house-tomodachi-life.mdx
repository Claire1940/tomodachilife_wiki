## Understanding the Two Types of Housing

Before diving into specific numbers, it is important to distinguish between the two types of residential structures on your island. Tomodachi Life uses a dual-housing system that changes based on the relationship status of your Miis.

### 1. The Mii Apartments
The Mii Apartments is the very first building you interact with. Every Mii you create or import starts here. This building is the central hub for single Miis and those who are dating but not yet married. The apartment building itself can be expanded several times as your population grows.

### 2. Mii Homes (The "Houses")
The Mii Homes area is unlocked only after two of your Miis get married. When a couple ties the knot, they move into a private house. However, they technically keep their original apartment rooms as well. This is a unique mechanic where Miis will spend time in both locations, though their "official" residence for family matters is the house.

## How Many People Can Live in One House Tomodachi Life?

When specifically looking at the Mii Homes (the individual houses), the answer is strictly defined by the game's family mechanics. A single house is designed to accommodate **one married couple**. This means two adult Miis are the permanent residents. 

However, the capacity temporarily increases when a couple has a child. During the nursing and toddler stages, the baby lives in the house with the parents. Therefore, at its peak, the answer to **how many people can live in one house tomodachi life** is three: two parents and one child.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Once a child reaches adulthood, they must leave the house. You will be given the choice to either move them into the Mii Apartments as a full-time resident or send them off to travel the world via StreetPass. They cannot stay in the family home indefinitely.

## Unlocking and Expanding Your Housing

To reach the maximum capacity of your island, you must consistently add new Miis and foster relationships. The apartment building does not start at its maximum size; it grows as you hit specific population milestones.

1.  **Initial Stage:** Holds up to 24 Miis.
2.  **First Expansion:** Occurs when you have 20+ Miis and have solved a certain number of problems.
3.  **Final Stage:** The building eventually reaches its 100-Mii limit.

For the Mii Homes area, you simply need to encourage romances. When a Mii has a "Love" icon (a pink heart), they may ask to propose. Successfully completing the proposal mini-game will result in a wedding and the immediate construction of a new house in the Mii Homes section.

## Strategies for Managing a Full Island

As you approach the 100-Mii limit, managing **how many people can live in one house tomodachi life** becomes a game of musical chairs. Since married Miis occupy both a house and two apartment slots, a high marriage rate doesn't actually "save" space in the apartment building.

### Tips for Island Efficiency:
*   **Monitor Relationships:** If your island is full, you may need to delete less-loved Miis to make room for children who want to move into the apartments.
*   **Encourage Traveling:** If you want to keep your apartment slots open for new, unique Miis, choose to send children off to travel rather than moving them into the building.
*   **Check the Town Hall:** Use the Town Hall to view a list of all residents and their current housing status. This is the easiest way to see who is living where.

## The Role of Children in Housing Capacity

The most dynamic aspect of the question **how many people can live in one house tomodachi life** involves the birth of children. Unlike other life sims where you can add multiple bedrooms, Tomodachi Life houses are static.

When a baby is born, the house becomes the center of all activity. You will be called to help soothe the baby, feed them, and play with them. During this week-long process, the "house" is functionally at its maximum capacity of three. 

> **💡 Tip:** If you have multiple married couples, they can all have babies simultaneously, provided you have enough space in your heart (and your schedule) to handle the mini-games!

### Child Growth Stages
1.  **Infant:** Stays in the house, requires constant attention.
2.  **Toddler:** Begins to move around the house, still lives with parents.
3.  **Adult:** The "Coming of Age" ceremony occurs, and the occupant limit for that specific house returns to two.

## FAQ

**Q: Can two Miis who aren't married live in the same house?**

A: No. In Tomodachi Life, only married couples can live in a house together. Single Miis, even those who are dating or "Best Friends," must maintain separate rooms in the Mii Apartments.

**Q: Does the house disappear if the Miis get divorced?**

A: Yes. If a married couple decides to divorce, the house in the Mii Homes area is removed. Both Miis will move back into their respective rooms in the Mii Apartments full-time.

**Q: How many people can live in one house tomodachi life if they have twins?**

A: Tomodachi Life does not feature twins or multiple births. Each pregnancy results in one child, meaning the maximum occupancy of a house never exceeds two adults and one baby at any given time.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many houses can be on the island?**

A: The limit is tied to your population. Since you can have a maximum of 100 Miis, and it takes two Miis to form a household, you could theoretically have up to 50 houses, provided every single Mii on your island is married to another resident.
