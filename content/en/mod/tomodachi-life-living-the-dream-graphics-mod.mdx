## Best Settings for Cleaner Pixel Results

A lot of players quit too early because the first conversion looks muddy. Usually, that’s a settings issue, not a skill issue.

### Use these baseline settings first

| Setting | Starter value | When to increase | When to decrease |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pixel resolution** | 32x32 or 48x48 | Large icons, mascots | Tiny accessories |
| **Color count** | 12–20 colors | Gradient-heavy art | Flat cartoon designs |
| **Dithering** | Low or off | Vintage texture style | Clean anime/cute style |
| **Edge sharpening** | Medium | Soft original images | Jagged outlines appear |
| **Palette mode** | Game palette first | If speed matters | Use custom only for accuracy |

If your converter offers a “game palette” mode, start there. It may look less exact than auto color, but it dramatically reduces matching time inside the editor.

> **Tip:** Prioritize silhouette and face readability over perfect color matching. In small grid art, shape clarity carries more visual impact than subtle shading.

### Practical color strategy

Use a 3-layer approach for most designs:
- **Base tone**
- **Shadow tone**
- **Highlight tone**

Then add one accent color for eyes, logos, or accessories. This keeps your **tomodachi life living the dream graphics mod** builds clean and recognizable even at small sizes.

## Advanced Techniques to Make Designs Look “Modded”

If you want your result to look closer to a true graphics overhaul, these advanced edits matter more than raw detail count.

### 1) Controlled contrast mapping
Boost difference between outline and fill by 10–20% (relative). Characters pop better against varied backgrounds.

### 2) Selective anti-jagging
Soften only key diagonal lines (hair edges, ears, curves). Don’t anti-jag every edge or your sprite can look blurry.

### 3) Palette anchoring
Use 2–3 recurring tones across multiple creations so your island’s custom content feels visually unified.

### 4) “Read-at-distance” check
Zoom out or step back from your screen. If eyes, mouth, and silhouette are clear, your design is ready.

| Technique | Visual benefit | Best for | Tradeoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Contrast mapping** | Strong readability | Faces, logos | Can look harsh if overdone |
| Selective anti-jagging | Smoother curves | Hair, clothing folds | Slightly longer editing time |
| Palette anchoring | Cohesive style set | Multi-character projects | Less color freedom |
| Distance check | Better in-play clarity | All designs | May reduce micro-detail |

When your friends ask how your assets look so polished, this is the difference-maker—not secret files, but disciplined visual decisions.

## Troubleshooting Common Problems

Even with good tools, players hit the same issues. Use this quick-fix table before restarting your project.

| Problem | Likely cause | Fast fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Design looks blurry** | Too much dithering or too many colors | Reduce colors, disable dithering |
| Colors don’t match in-game | Off-palette source tones | Re-convert using game palette mode |
| Edges look jagged | Resolution too low for subject | Increase grid size one step |
| Build takes too long | Over-detailed source image | Simplify before converting |
| Character unreadable in-game | Weak silhouette | Darker outline, stronger shape contrast |

> **Warning:** If a design is taking over 2 hours and still feels off, stop and simplify the source image first. Reworking complexity is faster than polishing a weak base.
