<Section icon={Image} title="Step-by-Step Custom Texture Import for Items">
Importing custom textures into *Tomodachi Life: Living The Dream* allows you to personalize various in-game items, adding a unique touch to your islanders' lives. This process typically involves a modding tool to convert and inject your chosen image files into the game's user-generated content (UGC) directory.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prepare Your Game and Texture">
    Start *Tomodachi Life: Living The Dream* and visit the Pallet House workshop. Create a new creation (e.g., a pet or food item) and add text to it for easy identification later. Purchase this item from the shop and give it to an islander. Crucially, **save your game** and then close the game completely.
    <Callout type="tip">
    Ensure your custom texture image is in **PNG format** and is typically square for best results. Non-PNG formats will not work with most texture import tools.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Download and Extract Modding Tool">
    Download a texture import tool like the Living the Dream Toolkit (available on platforms like GameBanana). If it's a zip file, use an archiving tool like 7-Zip to extract its contents to a convenient location.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Locate Your Game's UGC Folder">
    On Windows, press `Windows Key + R`, type `%appdata%`, and press Enter. Navigate to `Ryujinx/bis/user/save/0/UGC`. Copy this folder path. This is where your game's user-generated content, including the item you just created, is stored.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure the Texture Import Tool">
    Open the Living the Dream Toolkit (e.g., `Living the Dream ToolKit.GUI.exe`). In the tool, locate the "Open Save UGC Folder" option and paste the UGC folder path you copied in the previous step. Select the folder to load your save data.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Import and Convert Your Texture">
    Identify the item you created in Step 1 within the tool (it should be recognizable by the text you added). Click "Browse PNG" and select your custom PNG image. Note the "Item Type" and "Item ID" displayed by the tool (e.g., `goods`, `002`). Ensure these match the item you're modifying. Click "Convert" and then "Export".
    <Callout type="info">
    The "Item Type" and "Item ID" are crucial for correctly applying the texture to the desired in-game item. Double-check these details before converting and exporting.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Verify In-Game">
    Restart *Tomodachi Life: Living The Dream*. Go to your treasure shop, and while the item's name might still be the default, its texture should now display your custom image. Give the item to an islander to see the new texture in action.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Settings} title="Troubleshooting and Best Practices for Modding">
Engaging in **Tomodachi Life texture import** can be rewarding, but it's not without its challenges. Adhering to best practices and knowing how to troubleshoot common issues can ensure a smooth modding experience.

### Common Issues and Solutions

| Issue                               | Possible Cause                                      | Solution                                                                |
|:------------------------------------|:----------------------------------------------------|:------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Texture not appearing in-game**   | Incorrect file format, wrong item ID, improper save | Ensure PNG format, re-check item type/ID, save game correctly before tool use |
| **Game crashes after modding**      | Corrupted save file, incompatible mod, incorrect tool usage | Restore from backup, verify tool instructions, try a different mod      |
| **Mii face paint not transferring** | *Tomodachi Life* limitations, tool incompatibility | Use Miis with less complex face paint, or manually adjust in-game       |
| **Antivirus flags modding tool**    | New, independently compiled `.exe` files            | Add an exception to your antivirus or click "More info" and "Run anyway" (common false positive) |
| **Modding tool not finding UGC folder** | Incorrect path, game not saved properly             | Double-check `%appdata%` path, ensure game was saved and closed         |

<Callout type="warning" title="Antivirus Alerts">
Many new or independently developed modding tools may be flagged by Windows Defender or other antivirus software as a "SmartScreen" warning. This is often a false positive. If you trust the source, you can typically click "More info" and "Run anyway."
</Callout>
