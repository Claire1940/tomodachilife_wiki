## Getting Started: Initial Setup and Island Naming

Upon launching the demo, you will first be asked to select your region. This is a critical step as it is one of the few settings that cannot be changed later. Your region determines the default currency and certain localized food items (such as nachos in North America versus sushi in the UK). After confirming your system clock matches the real world, you are granted access to your barren island.

You have 27 characters to name your island, and for the first time, you can change the pronunciation of the name. If the text-to-speech engine mispronounces your creative title, you can manually adjust the phonetics to ensure it sounds exactly right during news broadcasts.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Choose your region carefully. While island names can be changed later in the Island Info menu, your geographical region and currency are locked to your save file.

## Mii Creation and the Updated Mii Maker

The foundation of any **tomodachi life demo gameplay guide** is the Mii creation process. In *Living the Dream*, the Mii Maker has received a massive overhaul, particularly in the "Face Painting" and "Pro Tools" sections. Players can now use layers (Paint Behind and Paint On Top) and even save custom face paint designs as stamps for future use.

### Personality Sliders
Your Mii's behavior is governed by five core sliders. Each slider operates on an 8-point scale, and the combination results in one of 16 distinct personality types, such as "Outgoing Dynamo" or "Easygoing Softie."

| Slider | Low End (1) | High End (8) |
|---|---|---|
| **Movement** | Slow & Deliberate | Quick & Energetic |
| **Speech** | Polite & Formal | Honest & Blunt |
| **Energy** | Flat & Calm | Varied & Expressive |
| **Thinking** | Serious & Logical | Chill & Intuitive |
| **Overall** | Normal | Quirky |

### New Identity Options
Nintendo has expanded the social identity features in this 2026 version. You can now select preferred pronouns (He/Him, She/Her, They/Them) and "Event Clothes." The latter allows you to decide if your Mii feels more comfortable in masculine or feminine formal wear for weddings and special dates, regardless of their gender selection.

## Island Progression: The Wishing Fountain

A major addition revealed in the demo is the **Wishing Fountain**. This serves as the central hub for island progression. By solving Mii "ponderings" (formerly known as problems), you earn a new currency called **Warm Fuzzies**. 

Feeding these Warm Fuzzies into the fountain levels up your island. Each level-up grants you a "Wish," which you can spend to unlock new features. In the demo, you can reach Island Level 2 and unlock basic categories like new walking styles or specific level-up gifts.

| Facility | Unlock Requirement (Demo) | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Fresh Kingdom** | Solve the first hunger problem | Purchase food items to raise happiness. |
| **Wishing Fountain** | Collect initial Warm Fuzzies | Level up the island and grant Wishes. |
| **Where & Wear** | Add a 3rd resident to the island | Purchase outfits and costumes. |
| **Right Price** | Obtain 5 different treasures | The island pawn shop for selling items. |

## Relationships and the Family Tree

The community's most requested feature has finally been implemented: **Family Trees**. When creating a Mii, you can now designate their relationship to existing islanders. Options include younger/older sibling, child, parent, and grandparent. 

Setting these relationships is a vital part of this **tomodachi life demo gameplay guide** because it hard-locks those Miis out of romantic interactions with one another. This prevents the "accidental romance" scenarios common in the original game. Miis will eventually "realize" their connection through gameplay, leading to unique cutscenes and dialogue.

> **💡 Tip:** To introduce two Miis quickly, physically pick one up in the apartment view and drop them onto another Mii. This forces an acquaintance meeting and starts their relationship tracker.

## Demo Limitations and Rewards

While the demo is generous, it does have specific caps to encourage purchasing the full game. Understanding these limits is key to managing your expectations during your playthrough.

*   **Resident Limit:** You can only add up to 3 residents in the demo version.
*   **Currency Cap:** You will stop earning money and Warm Fuzzies once you hit the demo's internal limit (usually after reaching Island Level 2).
*   **Shop Rotation:** Daily specials in the Food Mart and Clothing Store will only refresh once.
*   **Save Transfer:** All Miis, items, and progress carry over to the retail version.
*   **The Hamster Costume:** Completing the demo (unlocking the clothing store and solving basic problems) rewards you with a Hamster Costume in the full game. The color is randomized per save file.

## Advanced Customization: The Pro Editor

For those looking to create hyper-realistic Miis, the demo's Pro Editor is a game-changer. By utilizing the illumination scale and the wide color range, players have already recreated famous characters with startling detail. You can adjust shadows and highlights on face paint to create a 3D effect that wasn't possible on the 3DS.

If you are struggling to make a Mii from scratch, the "Get Help" feature is surprisingly effective. It uses a questionnaire about the person's features and personality to generate a base model that you can then fine-tune.

## FAQ

**Q: Does my progress in the Tomodachi Life demo gameplay guide carry over?**

A: Yes! All Miis created, items collected, and island levels achieved in the Welcome Version carry over directly to the full retail version of *Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream*.

**Q: How many Miis can I have in the demo?**

A: The demo is limited to 3 residents. However, the full game is expected to support significantly more, with island expansions unlocking as your population grows.

**Q: How do I unlock the Hamster Costume reward?**

A: You must "complete" the demo by unlocking the basic shops (Fresh Kingdom and Where & Wear) and reaching the point where the game presents a "Thank you for playing" screen. The costume will be available in your gift inventory in the full game.

**Q: Can I change a Mii's personality after I've created them?**

A: Yes, you can edit a Mii at any time through the "Edit Mii" option in their personal menu. This allows you to adjust their personality sliders, which will immediately change their behavior and dialogue style.
