## Tomodachi Life Demo All Features List: What’s Included?

The "Welcome Edition" is designed to give players a "small slice" of the island experience. While it restricts the number of residents you can have, it provides full access to the upgraded Mii Maker and several key facilities. Unlike previous iterations, your progress in this demo is not lost; it carries over directly to the full version of *Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream* upon its release.

## Advanced Mii Creation Tools

One of the most praised aspects of the new demo is the expanded Mii Maker. Players are no longer restricted to the basic system settings. The demo introduces two distinct paths for creation: "Get Help" and "From Scratch." The "Get Help" option is particularly useful for beginners, as it generates a character based on your answers to personality and aesthetic questions.

### Customization Upgrades
The customization depth in the 2026 demo far exceeds the original 3DS title. For the first time, players have access to a full color picker for every feature, allowing for truly unique skin tones and hair colors.

*   **Front and Back Hairstyles:** Hair is now split into sections (bangs and back), similar to the system used in the *Miitopia* Switch port.
*   **Face Paint Tool:** Use stamps or free-hand drawing to add details like freckles, scars, or artistic patterns.
*   **Alpha Lock:** A professional-grade toggle in the face paint menu that helps players design within specific boundaries.
*   **Inclusive Identity:** Set gender identity, pronouns (Male, Female, Nonbinary), and dating preferences independently.

## Social Dynamics and Relationships

Despite being a limited trial, the social engine is fully active in the demo. Miis can interact, form friendships, and even fall in love. The "Living the Dream" engine utilizes a new in-house text-to-speech system that makes Mii voices sound crisper and more natural than ever before.

| Social Mechanic | Demo Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Friendship** | Available | Miis bow to each other when meeting for the first time |
| **Romance** | Available | Characters can fall in love even with only 3 residents |
| **Island Dictionary** | Available | View and edit phrases you have taught your Miis |
| **Requests** | Available | Respond to hunger, boredom, or interior design needs |

A significant change in the 2026 version is that Miis can be interacted with anywhere on the island. You are no longer tethered to an apartment building; you might find your residents lounging on the beach or chatting on a patio, and you can jump into the conversation immediately.

## Regional Differences in the Demo

Eagle-eyed players have noticed that the demo varies slightly depending on your region. This suggests that Nintendo is maintaining the "regional charm" that made the original game famous, rather than a single global version.

*   **Food Items:** The European demo features different snacks (like Pain au chocolat) compared to the American version.
*   **Localization:** The "Giant Mii" dream sequence features different dialogue. In the NTSC version, the Mii asks, "Did you want to talk?" while the PAL version uses "You called?"
*   **Prices:** Some food prices have been adjusted for the 2026 economy. For example, Gummy Candy has increased from $0.50 to $1.00, while Ramen has actually decreased in price.

## Island Customization and Design

The demo introduces the **Palette House**, a new facility that allows for unprecedented creativity. While limited in the trial, this tool eventually allows players to design custom food, clothing, and even structures. 

1.  **Building Placement:** Unlike the fixed locations of the past, you can place houses and shops anywhere on the island map.
2.  **Quick Build:** A tool designed to help you develop the island infrastructure more efficiently.
3.  **Local Wireless:** You can connect with nearby friends to share Miis and custom items created in the Palette House.

> **Warning:** While you can share Miis via local wireless, the demo does not support the old QR code system from the 3DS, making Mii sharing slightly more manual.

## Post-Demo Gameplay: What Happens Next?

Once you have purchased your first item from the "Where & Wear" shop, the "story" of the demo concludes. You will receive a notification that you have unlocked the **Hamster Suit**, a special costume that will be waiting for you in the full game. 

However, the demo doesn't lock you out. You can continue to load your save and watch your three Miis live their lives. They will primarily stay in their houses and offer "tips" about the upcoming retail version. They will no longer gain experience points (Warm Fuzzies) or currency, but they will still exhibit the quirky behaviors and "Soap Opera" drama the series is known for.

For more official updates and to pre-order the full game, visit the [official Nintendo Store](https://www.nintendo.com) or check your local retailer.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the Tomodachi Life demo all features list include weddings?**

A: While weddings and babies are confirmed for the full game, they are extremely rare to trigger in the demo due to the three-Mii limit and the short gameplay loop. Most players will only see the initial stages of romance.

**Q: Can I change my Mii's personality after creating them in the demo?**

A: Yes, you can adjust personality traits and voices at any time. The demo also allows you to change pronouns and dating preferences without restarting your progress.

**Q: How do I get the hamster suit in the full game?**

A: After completing the demo objectives, your save file is flagged. When you boot up the full version of *Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream* on the same console, the game will detect your demo progress and add the suit to your inventory automatically.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many times I can play the demo?**

A: No, the "Welcome Edition" on Nintendo Switch does not have a launch limit. You can play and interact with your three Miis as much as you like until the full game launches.
