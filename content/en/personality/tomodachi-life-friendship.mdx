<Section icon={Users} title="How to Initiate and Level Up Tomodachi Life Friendship">

Forcing Miis to interact requires patience, but you can actively influence their social circles. The most common way to build a new **tomodachi life friendship** is by responding to the orange bubble prompts that appear in apartment windows. When a Mii has an orange icon, it indicates a social problem: they either want to make a new friend, introduce two existing friends, or ask your advice on how to approach someone they are curious about.

When introducing two Miis, the game presents you with four conversation topics. Choosing the right topic is crucial, as it sets the tone for their initial interaction. If the conversation goes well, stars and sparkles will appear around the Miis, signaling a successful bond. If it fails, they will part ways as acquaintances without establishing a deeper connection.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Managing Best Friends and Roommates">

Once a **tomodachi life friendship** is established, continuous positive interactions can upgrade it to "Best Friend" status. Best friends are incredibly valuable assets on your island. They get along significantly better than standard friends, rarely engage in severe arguments, and unlock special joint activities. Furthermore, when two Miis become best friends, they can be utilized to resolve massive fights between other islanders.

In advanced community concepts like *Living the Dream*, the friendship system extends to shared living arrangements. Friends or relatives who maintain an exceptionally high relationship level over a long period can request to move into a shared house as roommates. This unique mechanic allows you to build custom households, further diversifying the social dynamics of your island.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Resolving Fights and Avoiding the Ex-Friends State">

Not all social interactions on your island are peaceful. Miis will frequently get into disagreements, marked by a red scribble bubble or a gray worry bubble. If a Mii is angry, they may pace around their room, throw household items, or outright refuse to speak to the person who offended them. Your intervention as the island creator is required to soothe their anger and prompt an apology.

If a fight is left unresolved, or if an apology attempt fails miserably, the relationship level will drop drastically. In standard gameplay, they may simply become distant. However, in more complex relationship mods, failing to reconcile can permanently turn them into "Ex-Friends." Ex-Friends will actively avoid each other, run away if placed in the same room, and refuse to participate in joint island events.

<Callout type="warning" title="Calming Angry Miis">
Never try to force an angry Mii to apologize while they are still actively throwing objects or venting. Always give them a calming item first, such as a music box, a bath set, or their favorite comfort food, to lower their stress level.
</Callout>
