<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Step-by-Step Character Construction">

Creating a recognizable character requires more than just picking the right hair. It involves manipulating the "hidden" sliders for rotation and stretch. By following a structured workflow, you can ensure that the proportions remain realistic even when using exaggerated features for anime-style characters.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select Base Features">
    Choose the gender and basic head shape. For younger characters or anime styles, a rounded jawline is generally more effective. Set the skin tone immediately, as this affects how eye shadows and mouth colors appear.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Map the Eyes and Brows">
    This is the most critical step. Use the distance slider to determine if the character looks focused or "derpy." For characters like Hatsune Miku, increasing the size and slightly rotating the eyes upward creates a classic "moe" aesthetic.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Refine the Nose and Mouth">
    In many stylized designs, the nose is minimized. Shrink it to the smallest size and move it up toward the eyes. Select a mouth shape that reflects the character's typical mood (e.g., a small smile for "Easygoing" types).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Hair and Accessories">
    Layer eyebrows over hair if necessary to create "bangs" or extra detail. If the character wears glasses, choose a frame that doesn't obscure the eye expressions you worked hard to create.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Proportion Trick">
If a Mii looks "off" but you can't tell why, try lowering the entire facial cluster (eyes, nose, mouth) by two clicks. A lower face often results in a more youthful and appealing character design.
</Callout>

</Section>
