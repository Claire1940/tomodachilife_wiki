<Section icon={Target} title="Method 1: The Overlay Technique">

The Overlay Technique is the fastest way to achieve **tomodachi life heterochromia**. This method involves using the existing Mii eyes as a template and simply painting a new color over one or both of them. This is ideal for players who want to keep the exact shape of the original Mii eyes without having to redraw them perfectly.

To begin, open the Mii face paint editor and select the smiling face icon located under the preview screen. You must enable the option to "draw on top of facial features." Once enabled, you can use the brush tool to color over the iris of one eye.

| Step | Action | Key Detail |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | Open Editor | Navigate to the Face Paint section of the Mii. |
| **2** | Enable Overlay | Click the smiling icon to allow drawing over eyes. |
| **3** | Trace Iris | Use a small brush to color over the existing iris. |
| **4** | Save & Test | Check the Mii in the apartment to see the effect. |

<Callout type="warning" title="The Blinking Glitch">
When using Method 1, the original Mii eyes still exist underneath your painting. When the Mii blinks, the painted eye will stay open while the eyelid moves *behind* or *underneath* it, which can look unnatural in close-ups.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Method 2: Drawing from Scratch">

For a cleaner and more professional result, many players prefer to draw the eyes entirely from scratch. This method solves the "double eye" visual glitch where the original eyes blink underneath the paint. By removing the default eyes entirely, you create a blank canvas for your **tomodachi life heterochromia** masterpiece.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Enable Feature Drawing">
    Enter the Face Paint editor and ensure the "draw on top of facial features" setting is toggled on. This is essential for the paint to be visible on the eye area.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Manual Tracing">
    Using the default eyes as a ghost guide, trace the entire eye shape, including the white sclera, the iris, and the pupil. Use the zoom function for better precision.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Color Application">
    Apply your two different colors to the left and right irises. Since you are drawing manually, you can even add gradients or unique highlights that aren't possible in the base game.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Remove Default Eyes">
    Exit the editor and go to the standard Mii "Eyes" tab. Select the "None" or "Hidden" eye type. Now, only your hand-drawn heterochromia eyes will be visible.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Element | Brush Size | Recommended Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sclera (White)** | Medium | Fill the base shape first to cover the skin. |
| **Iris Color A** | Small | Draw a perfect circle on the left side. |
| **Iris Color B** | Small | Draw a perfect circle on the right side. |
| **Pupils** | Fine | Add a black dot in the center of both. |
| **Highlights** | Fine | Add a small white dot for a "living" look. |

<Callout type="success" title="Best Result">
Drawing from scratch is the preferred method for high-quality Mii creation. It prevents any clipping issues and allows for custom eye shapes that the vanilla game doesn't offer.
