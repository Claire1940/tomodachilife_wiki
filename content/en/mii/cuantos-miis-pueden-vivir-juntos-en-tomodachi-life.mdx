## How to Trigger Miis Moving In Together

Unlike many other simulation games, you do not have direct "drag and drop" control over where your Miis live. You cannot simply force two Miis into a house because you think they would be good roommates. Instead, the progression is entirely organic and based on the AI's relationship status. To understand **cuantos miis pueden vivir juntos en tomodachi life**, you must first master the two primary ways Miis decide to share a roof.

### 1. The Marriage Path
This is the most common and reliable way to see Miis living together. When two Miis are in a romantic relationship and their "Relationship" meter reaches a high enough level (typically "Let's get married!" or "Soulmates"), one Mii will eventually ask for your help to propose. Once the wedding ceremony is complete, the game automatically generates a house in the "Mii Homes" section of the island. The couple will spend their nights there, though they still keep their individual apartments for daytime activities and item management.

### 2. The Roommate Path
A lesser-known feature involves "Best Friends" deciding to become roommates. If two Miis of the same gender (or occasionally different genders, depending on the version and relationship status) become exceptionally close, they may approach you with the idea of living together. This is more random than the marriage path and requires frequent check-ins. If you approve the request, they will move into a shared space.

> **💡 Tip:** To increase the chances of Miis moving in together, give them similar items, foods, and hobbies to encourage frequent interactions in each other's apartments.

## Expanding the Household to the 8-Mii Limit

Once a house is established, it doesn't have to stay limited to just two people. This is where the question of **cuantos miis pueden vivir juntos en tomodachi life** becomes interesting. The household can grow through several natural game events.

### Family Growth and Children
If a married couple has a high relationship rating, they will eventually ask if they should have a baby. If you agree, a child will be born and live in the house with them. As the child grows, they remain part of that household unit until they are fully grown. At that point, you can choose to let them move into their own apartment or send them off to travel the world via StreetPass.

### Group Living Dynamics
As seen in advanced gameplay scenarios, it is possible to have multiple roommates and family members under one roof. For example, if a group of roommates is living together and one of them gets married, the game may offer the choice to keep the new spouse in the existing shared home rather than moving them to a new one. This layering of relationships is the only way to hit the upper limit of 8 residents.

## Strategies for Managing Large Households

Managing a house with many residents requires more attention than individual apartments. You will find that the Miis in shared housing have more complex social needs and problems. If you are trying to maximize **cuantos miis pueden vivir juntos en tomodachi life**, you should follow these expert strategies:

1.  **Frequent Check-ins:** Miis in shared houses trigger events more often. If you ignore them, their relationship levels can drop, leading to Miis moving back out to individual apartments.
2.  **Solve Problems Promptly:** When a Mii has a "bubble" over their house, prioritize it. These problems often involve the other residents, and solving them strengthens the bond of the entire house.
3.  **Use the Compatibility Tester:** Check the [official Nintendo support pages](https://www.nintendo.com/) or in-game tools to see which Miis are most likely to succeed as roommates or spouses before encouraging them to move in.
4.  **Gift Shared Interests:** Giving residents of the same house the same Wii U console or the same set of Wii Sports gear will cause them to play together more frequently, stabilizing the household.

> **⚠️ Warning:** If a married couple gets divorced or roommates have a massive falling out, the shared house will be deleted, and the Miis will return to their separate apartments immediately.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I manually move a Mii into another Mii's house?**

A: No, you cannot manually move them. You must wait for the Miis to ask for your permission to live together based on their relationship status. Your role is limited to encouraging their friendship and approving the move when the prompt appears.

**Q: What is the absolute limit for cuantos miis pueden vivir juntos en tomodachi life in one building?**

A: For the shared "Mii Homes," the limit is 8 Miis. For the main Apartment Building, the limit is 100 Miis, but they each live in their own individual units there.

**Q: Do children count toward the 8-Mii limit?**

A: Yes, children are considered residents of the household. If a house already has a large number of roommates and spouses, you may find that the option to have a baby is temporarily disabled until someone moves out.

**Q: Can three friends live together as roommates?**

A: Yes, but this usually happens sequentially. Two Miis move in together first, and then a third Mii who has a very high relationship with both residents may eventually ask to join the household.
