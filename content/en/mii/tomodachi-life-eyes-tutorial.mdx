Similarly, altering the rotation can completely change the expression of the Mii, turning a neutral face into one that looks constantly surprised, angry, or sleepy.

<Section icon={Target} title="Tomodachi Life Eyes Tutorial: Step-by-Step Customization Process">

Follow this systematic approach to design custom eyes for your islanders. This step-by-step process ensures you balance proportions before finalizing fine details like color and tilt.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Select the Base Eye Shape">
    Choose a base shape from the page menu. Look for shapes that match the overall style you want to achieve. Round shapes work best for cute characters, while angular lines suit serious looks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust the Vertical Height and Spacing">
    Position the eyes vertically on the face. Keep them relatively centered. Adjust the spacing slider so there is roughly one eye-width of space between them for a natural look.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Fine-Tune Size and Rotation">
    Scale the eyes up or down. Rotate them slightly inward to create a friendly appearance, or outward for a cooler, more distant look. Small adjustments here make a significant difference.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Match with Eyebrows and Accessories">
    Align the eyebrows with the top of the eyes. Eyebrows can frame the eyes and hide raw edges of certain eye shapes, creating the illusion of custom eyelashes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once you complete these steps, test the expressions in the Mii Maker preview window. The game animations will stretch and distort the eyes during dialogue, so ensuring they look good in motion is just as important as their static appearance.

<Callout type="warning" title="Warning">
Extreme rotation values can cause the eyes to clip through glasses or specific hair styles during talking animations.
</Callout>

Creating distinct aesthetics requires looking beyond the basic presets. By combining eye shapes with specific eyebrow types, you can create entirely new facial structures. This technique is especially useful for recreating anime characters or real-life celebrities.

To make your characters look unique, experiment with the interaction between the eye and the nose. Lowering the eyes closer to the nose creates a more youthful, stylized look, which is common in modern character design. Conversely, placing them higher up gives the character a longer, more mature face shape.

<Callout type="success" title="Design Tip">
Use the dot-style nose and position it slightly between the eyes to mimic classic Japanese cartoon styles.
</Callout>
