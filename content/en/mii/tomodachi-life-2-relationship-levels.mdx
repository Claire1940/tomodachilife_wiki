Use this as a repeatable cycle each session.

## Best Event Management: From Crush to Confession

Once a crush appears, event handling matters more than raw interaction count. This is the phase where many players lose momentum by ignoring prompts.

### What to do when a Mii confesses feelings
- Encourage constructive choices.
- Keep both Miis socially active right after the scene.
- Avoid long inactivity gaps for either character.

### What to do with one-sided crushes
- Keep contact flowing; don’t instantly abandon the pair.
- If repeated failures happen, pause and shift effort to a different target for a while.
- Revisit the original pair after broader social changes on the island.

### What to do after dating starts
- Continue routine interactions.
- Watch for conflict signs and stabilize with positive social support.
- Build island rhythm so the couple appears in shared social moments often.

## Advanced Strategy for Multi-Couple Islands

As your island grows, it gets harder to control every pairing. Use a priority framework.

### Priority Framework
1. **Primary pair**: Your current romance target
2. **Secondary pair**: Backup if primary stalls
3. **Maintenance pairs**: Existing couples you want to preserve

This keeps your schedule efficient and avoids “social noise” overwhelming your key target.

| Island Phase | Main Goal | Recommended Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Early Island** | Create first couple | 1 focused pair |
| **Mid Island** | Expand romance network | 1 primary + 1 secondary pair |
| **Late Island** | Stability and milestone events | Couple maintenance + selective new pair attempts |

You can also rotate priorities every few in-game days. That prevents burnout on one stubborn pair while still preserving progress patterns.

> **Tip:** If a couple formed recently, give them maintenance attention before pushing a new romance hard. Stable couples are easier to keep than to rebuild.
