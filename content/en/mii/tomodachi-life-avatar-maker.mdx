<Section icon={Map} title="Mastering Face Paint Mode (Easy vs. Pro Mode)">

One of the most revolutionary additions in the 2026 sequel is the dedicated Face Paint Mode. In previous games, drawing on a Mii's face was a temporary prank limited to when they were sleeping. In *Living the Dream*, face paint has been redesigned into a permanent customization mechanic within the **tomodachi life avatar maker**.

When entering the Face Paint interface, you can choose between **Easy Mode** and **Pro Mode** (known as Artist Mode in European regions). Easy Mode provides a streamlined set of tools for quick additions, while Pro Mode unlocks advanced features, layering, and a 100-color palette.

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: Creating a Cute, Expressive Mii">

Creating a Mii that looks both stylized and highly expressive requires a blend of built-in features and custom face paint. While you can draw an entire face from scratch, doing so means your Mii won't blink or emote. Follow this step-by-step tutorial to design a Mii that retains its interactive animations while looking completely unique.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Start from Scratch">
    Select "Create from Scratch" in the Town Hall menu. Choose your baseline skin tone and hair color. You can press the X button to toggle different default skin tones before you begin sculpting.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Sculpt the Eyes and Eyebrows">
    Position the eyebrows lower than their default setting and make them slightly darker than the hair for contrast. Use the eye crease details to frame the eyes; this ensures your Mii still blinks and emotes dynamically when happy or shocked.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Mix and Match Hairstyles">
    Select the bangs and the back of the hair separately. Add a secondary hair color (sub-color) or highlights to create depth. This eliminates the need for the single-use hair color sprays from previous games.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Apply Custom Face Paint">
    Switch to Pro Mode in the Face Paint menu. Use a soft brush with a darker, pinkish hue to paint realistic blush on the cheeks. Add a small vertical shadow line in the center of the face to act as a simplified, cute nose.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Configure Voice and Personality">
    Adjust the robotic voice sliders (pitch, speed, and accent) to match your character's vibe. Assign their gender, pronouns, and dating preferences, then complete the personality test to set their social behavior.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Depixelation Feature">
Do not worry if your custom lines look pixelated while drawing on the face paint canvas. The game automatically depixelates and smoothes out your strokes once you save and exit the editor, resulting in clean, HD vector lines.
</Callout>

</Section>
