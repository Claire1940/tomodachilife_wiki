Apartment life is driven by "Problem Bubbles." These icons appear in the apartment windows on the island map, signaling that a Mii needs your attention. Ignoring these bubbles halts your progression, as they are the primary source of experience points (Happiness) and money.

| Bubble Color | Meaning | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Black** | General Problem (Hunger, Clothing, Games) | High |
| **Orange** | Social Interaction (Friendship, Rivalry) | Medium |
| **Pink** | Romance (Confessions, Proposals, Dates) | Critical |
| **Green** | Mini-game Request (Pixel Quiz, Matchmaker) | Low |
| **Blue** | Sadness (Breakups, Failed Confessions) | High |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Management Tip">
Always prioritize Pink and Blue bubbles. Pink bubbles drive the marriage system, while Blue bubbles prevent your Miis from becoming depressed, which stops all other social progression for that character.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Unlocking Apartment Facilities and Shops">

Your journey with **tomodachi life apartments** begins with a nearly empty island. To make the building a true home, you must unlock various facilities by interacting with your residents. Most shops are triggered by solving a specific number of problems or reaching population milestones.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Establish the Resident Base">
    Create your first Mii (the look-alike) to open the apartments. Immediately add at least one male and one female Mii to enable social triggers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Solve Initial Problems">
    Focus on the black bubbles. Once you solve 5 problems and have 1 male and 1 female resident, the **Clothing Store** will unlock, allowing you to change resident outfits.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Expand the Catalog">
    Reach 10 solved problems and ensure you have at least 2 male and 2 female Miis. This triggers the opening of the **Hat Store**, further expanding customization options.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Unlock the Interior Shop">
    The **Interiors Store** is crucial for apartment customization. It unlocks once two Miis have saved at least $200 each. You can speed this up by giving Miis high-value items or expensive food.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Facility | Unlock Requirement | Primary Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Food Mart** | Automatically after first Mii asks for food | Buying daily meals and snacks |
| **Rankings Board** | 5 Miis + 15 Solved Problems | Tracking popularity and compatibility |
| **Mii News** | 7 Miis (at least 3 of each gender) | Random comedic events and updates |
| **Compatibility Tester** | 2M/2F Miis + 30 Solved Problems | Predicting friendship and romance |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stock Rotation">
Shop stock in Tomodachi Life rotates daily at midnight. If you see a rare interior or outfit, buy it immediately! Once purchased, it is permanently added to your "Buy Again" catalog.
</Callout>

</Section>

Relationships in the apartments follow a specific hierarchy. Understanding these levels helps you predict when a Mii might ask to propose or when a breakup is imminent.

| Relationship Status | Description | Outcome Risk |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Great Pal** | Strong friendship between any two Miis | Low fight risk |
| **Sweetheart** | Romantic bond between opposite genders | Proposal potential |
| **Soul Mate** | The highest romantic tier | Guaranteed marriage success |
| **Not Getting Along** | Negative momentum in friendship | High fight/rivalry risk |

<Callout type="info" title="The Marriage Move-Out">
When two Miis living in the apartments get married, they will move into a private house in the **Mii Homes** section. However, they keep their original apartment rooms for "me time," meaning you can still interact with them in both locations.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Leveling Up and Rewards">

Every time you solve a problem in the **tomodachi life apartments**, the Mii gains experience. When the bar fills, they level up, and you can grant them a permanent gift. These gifts expand the Mii's capabilities and the variety of animations you see in the building.

| Level Reward | Effect on Apartment Life |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Songs** | Unlocks the Concert Hall; Miis perform in their rooms |
| **Gifts** | Items like Wii U, VR Headsets, or Frying Pans for room use |
| **Interiors** | Change the room style for free |
| **Catchphrases** | Miis will say these during News segments or visits |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Maximize Food Interaction">
    Feed Miis their favorite foods to gain the most XP. A "Super All-Time Favorite" food will launch the Mii into space and nearly fill an entire level bar.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Utilize Items">
    Give Miis items like the "Slide Puzzle" or "Caleidoscope." Watching them use these in their apartments generates small amounts of XP and money over time.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Strategic Gifting">
    At higher levels, prioritize giving Miis "Travel Tickets." This is the only way to significantly boost a relationship between two Miis who are struggling to get along.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Hidden Treasure">
Occasionally, Miis will offer you a "Treasure" after you solve a problem. These can be sold at the Pawn Shop for significant cash, which you can then reinvest into expensive interiors for your favorite residents.
</Callout>
