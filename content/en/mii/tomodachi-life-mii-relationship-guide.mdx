## Understanding Relationship Levels and Statuses

In Tomodachi Life, every Mii maintains a specific status with every other Mii they have met. You can track these by checking the "Relationships" tab on a Mii’s profile. The game uses color-coded phrases to indicate how well two Miis are getting along. These range from "Unsure" to "Soulmate" for romantic partners, and "Not Getting Along" to "Best Buddy" for friends.

| Status Type | Relationship Label | Meaning & Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Friendship** | Best Buddy | The highest level of non-romantic friendship; likely to suggest moving in. |
| **Friendship** | Great Buddy | A strong bond where Miis frequently visit each other's apartments. |
| **Romantic** | Soulmate | The peak of romantic affection; almost guaranteed a successful proposal. |
| **Romantic** | Let's Get Married! | The Mii is actively thinking about proposing to their partner. |
| **Negative** | Not Getting Along | High risk of a fight or the relationship dissolving entirely. |

To improve these statuses, you must encourage Miis to spend time together. While you cannot force a Mii to like another, you can influence the frequency of their meetings by giving them similar items, such as Wii Us or travel tickets, which often trigger joint cutscenes.

## How to Make Miis Live Together

One of the most sought-after features in the 2026 "Living the Dream" update and the base game is the ability for Miis to share a home. Unlike the standard apartments where every Mii lives solo, shared housing allows for a more communal experience. However, you cannot simply drag and drop a Mii into another's house. The process is entirely organic and driven by the relationship progression.

### Method 1: Marriage
Marriage is the most reliable way to get Miis to live together. When two Miis are in a romantic relationship and their affection reaches a high enough level, a "Love" bubble (a pink heart) may appear in their window. This indicates they are thinking about a proposal. Once the proposal mini-game is successfully completed, the couple will marry and automatically move into a private house on the island.

### Method 2: Roommates (Best Friends)
If two Miis reach the status of "Best Buddy," they may eventually approach you with the idea of becoming roommates. This is a rarer event than marriage but functions similarly. When the prompt appears, you must approve the request for them to move into a shared space.

> **💡 Tip:** To increase the chances of a roommate request, ensure both Miis have high happiness levels and frequently give them the same food or gifts to encourage "shared interest" flags in the game's logic.

## Expanding the Household

Once a house is established—whether through marriage or friendship—it doesn't have to stop at just two residents. A single house can eventually hold up to eight Miis. This is particularly useful for creating large family units or "group homes" for your favorite characters.

To add more Miis to an existing household, the new Mii must have a very strong relationship with at least one person already living in the house. As you continue to play, the game may offer choices during major life events. For example, if a roommate gets married, you can choose to have the new spouse move into the existing group home or start a new house entirely. Similarly, when children are born or added via the profile settings, you can opt to keep the entire family under one roof.

| Household Size | Requirement | Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 Mii** | Default | Standard apartment living; easy to manage. |
| **2 Miis** | Marriage or Best Friends | Unlocks the Mii House area; unique interactions. |
| **3-8 Miis** | Family or Close Bonds | Maximum social interaction; saves space on the island map. |

## Boosting Affection and Compatibility

The core of any **tomodachi life mii relationship guide** is the "Compatibility Tester." While the percentage shown in the tester (based on birthdays) doesn't strictly dictate whether Miis *can* date, it does influence how quickly their affection grows. A 90% compatibility rating means they will likely resolve fights faster and gain affection points more rapidly during conversations.

### Top Ways to Increase Mii Bonds:
1.  **Travel Tickets:** Giving a Mii a travel ticket when they are with a friend will send both on a vacation, significantly boosting their relationship.
2.  **Problem Solving:** Always address the "orange" thought bubbles. Helping a Mii with a social dilemma (like making a new friend) is the primary way to expand their social circle.
3.  **Gifts and Hobbies:** Give Miis items like the "Disposable Camera" or "Kite." If two Miis use these items together, their bond strengthens.
4.  **Food Preferences:** While food mostly affects happiness, a Mii who is well-fed and happy is more likely to have positive social interactions than one who is neglected.

> **Warning:** If a Mii has a "broken heart" icon (a blue jagged heart), their relationship has ended. You must help them recover by providing their favorite foods or entertaining them before they will be open to new relationships.

## Managing Conflict and Breakups

Not every relationship on the island is destined for success. Miis will occasionally have "fire" bubbles in their windows, indicating a major fight. If you don't intervene by calming the Mii down (usually by giving them a bath or their favorite snack), the relationship can take a permanent hit.

If a relationship is failing, you might see a "cloudy" thought bubble where a Mii expresses doubt about their partner or friend. You can choose to tell them to "Try harder" or "Give up." If you are following a specific **tomodachi life mii relationship guide** strategy to keep a couple together, always encourage them to work through it, though this is not always successful.

| Conflict Level | Visual Cue | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minor Spat** | Orange Bubble | Mediate the conversation between the two Miis. |
| **Major Fight** | Fire Bubble | Give the Mii a **Music Box** or **Bath Set** immediately. |
| **Depression** | Blue Bubble | Feed them their **Super All-Time Favorite** food to clear the gloom. |

## FAQ

**Q: Can I force two Miis to get married?**

A: No, you cannot force a marriage. You can only encourage the relationship by ensuring they interact often and by successfully completing the proposal mini-game when the Mii initiates it.

**Q: How do I unlock the Compatibility Tester?**

A: The Compatibility Tester is unlocked after you have at least two male and two female Miis on your island and have performed a certain number of social interactions. It is essential for any **tomodachi life mii relationship guide** enthusiast to check this regularly.

**Q: What is the maximum number of Miis that can live in one house?**

A: A single house can accommodate up to eight Miis, provided they have the necessary family or friendship links to justify the shared living space.

**Q: Can Miis get divorced?**

A: Yes, if a married couple's relationship status drops significantly and they fail to reconcile after a fight, they may ask for a divorce. This will result in the house being removed and both Miis moving back into separate apartments.
