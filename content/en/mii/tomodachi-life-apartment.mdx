<Section icon={Map} title="Mii Apartments Overview & Upgrade Milestones">

The **tomodachi life apartment** building serves as the central hub and primary residence for all Miis on your island. In the classic simulation game, every Mii you create or import moves directly into this multi-story complex. It is the only location where you can fully interact with your islanders, check on their daily thoughts, and solve their personal problems to gain rewards. Whether they are craving a specific snack, feeling lonely, or looking for relationship advice, you will spend the majority of your gameplay loop tapping on these colorful windows to manage their daily lives.

As your island population grows, the apartment building undergoes several automatic upgrades. You do not need to spend any in-game currency or resources to expand the building; the expansion triggers purely based on the number of registered islanders. Each upgrade not only increases the room capacity but also completely remodels the exterior design of the building, turning it from a basic brick structure into an elegant golden landmark.

| Upgrade Stage | Islander Requirement | Max Room Capacity | Exterior Style |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Initial Stage** | **0 Islanders** | **24 Miis** | Standard look, basic lobby |
| **First Upgrade** | **20 Islanders** | **48 Miis** | Brownish brick-like appearance |
| **Second Upgrade** | **45 Islanders** | **72 Miis** | Grayish, stony-like material |
| **Final Upgrade** | **70 Islanders** | **100 Miis** | Elegant gold with front gate and trees |

Once you reach the final upgrade at 70 islanders, the building can hold up to 100 Miis in total. At this stage, the top four apartments are positioned directly in the center of the top floor, giving those rooms a premium penthouse aesthetic. This visual progression provides a satisfying sense of growth as you transform your quiet island into a bustling metropolis.

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip: Fast Expansion">
To reach the final upgrade quickly, use the Town Hall to scan Mii QR codes or create quick look-alikes. Reaching 70 islanders unlocks the ultimate golden aesthetic, making your island look incredibly prestigious.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Interactive Mii Behaviors & Room Activities">

Inside their individual rooms, your Miis will engage in a wide variety of autonomous behaviors. They do not simply stand idle; they live their lives according to their unique personalities, moods, and the current season. You might find them skipping happily across the floor, rolling around in a fit of worry, or doing intense physical exercises like push-ups and crunches.

These behaviors are heavily influenced by their personality types. Confident and Outgoing Miis are much more likely to be seen performing active workouts, while Easygoing or Relaxed Miis might spend their time staring at the ceiling or taking long naps. Seasonal changes also affect their actions; during the hot summer months, Miis will fan themselves to stay cool, while during the winter, they will shiver and blow warm air into their hands.

| Activity | Mood/Season Trigger | Mii Behavior |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Rolling on Floor** | **Normal, Happy, Worried** | Mii rolls back and forth on the carpet |
| **Fanning Themselves** | **Summer Season** | Mii uses a hand fan to cool down |
| **Breathing into Hands** | **Winter Season** | Mii warms up their hands with breath |
| **Push-ups & Crunches** | **Confident, Outgoing** | Mii performs active physical exercises |
| **Hiccupping** | **Random Trigger** | Mii acts Outgoing and hiccups continuously |

One of the most unique and humorous mechanics in the **tomodachi life apartment** is the hiccup state. At random times, a Mii may develop a persistent case of the hiccups. When this happens, their behavior temporarily shifts to Outgoing, regardless of their actual personality. If you enter their apartment while they are hiccupping, you will startle them, triggering a hilarious jump animation.

<Section icon={Target} title="Apartment Office Features & Management">

At the very bottom of the apartment building screen, you will find the Office. The Office is the administrative hub for your island. Tapping on it does not open a Mii's room; instead, it opens a suite of management tools and statistical readouts that help you keep track of your island's overall health and demographics.

The top screen of the Office displays crucial information split across three pages. Page 1 shows your general community vibe, the current islander count, the average age of your residents, the male-to-female ratio, and the total number of problems solved. Page 2 tracks major milestones such as the number of weddings held, children born, and travelers sent out. Page 3 displays your chosen export item.

The bottom screen offers two primary interactive features: the Personality Chart and Apartment Swap. The Personality Chart organizes all your Miis into a grid based on their personality classifications, making it easy to see if your island leans more toward outgoing adventurers or quiet thinkers. The Apartment Swap tool is particularly useful for players who want to keep their building organized.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access the Office">
    Tap the Office icon located at the very bottom of the apartment building screen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Select Apartment Swap">
    Choose the Apartment Swap option on the bottom touch screen to open the grid layout of your building.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select the First Mii">
    Tap on the room of the Mii you wish to relocate. This highlights their current position.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Choose the Target Room">
    Tap on another Mii's room or an empty slot to swap their locations instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Confirm and Save">
    Review the new building layout and confirm the swap to save your organizational changes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Organization Tip">
While swapping rooms does not affect friendship levels or romantic relationships, moving compatible Miis near each other can make managing their daily schedules slightly more convenient and visually satisfying.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Apartment Captions & Status Meanings">

When you look at the grid of the **tomodachi life apartment** building, you will notice different captions appearing next to each Mii's name. These captions are incredibly useful because they tell you exactly what the Mii is doing without requiring you to enter their room. It is a highly efficient way to scan the building for social events, conflicts, or empty rooms.

These captions vary slightly depending on whether you are playing the North American (US) or European (EU) version of the game. For example, when a Mii is relaxing inside, the US version reads "[Mii] is home," while the EU version reads "[Mii] is in." Understanding these captions helps you identify when a Mii is visiting a friend, working a part-time job, or fast asleep.

| US Caption | EU Caption | Meaning & Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **[Mii] is home.** | **[Mii] is in.** | The Mii is relaxing in their apartment |
| **[Mii 1] is hanging out with [Mii 2].** | **[Mii 1] is in here with [Mii 2].** | A visitor is currently in the apartment |
| **[Mii] is at [his/her] house.** | **[Mii] is in [his/her] house.** | Married Mii is at their shared Mii Home |
| **[Mii] is fast asleep and didn't notice.** | **[Mii] seems to be fast asleep...** | Mii is in a deep sleep; cannot be woken up |
| **[Mii] is working at part-time job.** | **[Mii] is out working...** | Mii is acting as a shopkeeper |

If a Mii gets married, they will move into their own house in the Mii Homes section of the island. However, they do not lose their room in the apartment building. Instead, their apartment remains reserved for them, and they will frequently return to it to sleep, think, or host friends. If you try to call a Mii who is fast asleep at their house, you will see a caption stating they are deep in sleep and cannot hear you.

<Callout type="warning" title="Sleeping Restrictions">
If a Mii is fast asleep, you cannot interact with them or give them items. You will have to wait until they wake up naturally or enter their dream to interact.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Evolution: Apartments vs. Living the Dream Housing">

The landscape of Mii social simulations changed forever with the global release of *Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream* on April 16, 2026, for the Nintendo Switch and Switch 2. This highly anticipated sequel introduced massive upgrades to the classic formula, most notably in how housing is handled.

While the original game relied entirely on the static, multi-story **tomodachi life apartment** building, *Living the Dream* replaces this structure with individual and shared houses. Instead of being confined to a single high-rise, players can now place houses and shops anywhere on the island map. Furthermore, up to eight Miis can share a single house, allowing for complex roommate dynamics, custom families, and themed households that were never possible in the 3DS era.

| Feature | Tomodachi Life (3DS) | Living the Dream (Switch, 2026) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary Housing** | **Mii Apartments (Up to 100 Miis)** | Customizable Individual/Shared Houses |
| **Max Residents Per Unit** | **1 Mii per apartment** | Up to 8 Miis per house |
| **Map Placement** | **Fixed central location** | Placeable anywhere on the island map |
| **Married Couples** | **Move to separate Mii Homes** | Can live together or with up to 6 others |
| **Customization Scale** | **Interior wallpapers only** | Full exterior, interior, and layout control |
