## The Foundations of Mii Romance

Before you can start playing matchmaker, you must understand the hard rules that govern Mii relationships. Unlike friendship, which is relatively open, romantic love has specific eligibility requirements that the game strictly enforces. If these conditions aren't met, your Miis will never progress past being "just friends."

### Eligibility and Restrictions
The most important factor is the age group. In Tomodachi Life, Miis are categorized as either "Kids" (under 18) or "Adults" (18 and over) based on the birth year you enter. An Adult Mii cannot date a Kid Mii under any circumstances. If you want two Miis to fall in love, ensure they are in the same age bracket.

Additionally, in the updated "Living the Dream" version, dating preferences play a massive role. You can now set specific orientations for your Miis, including preferences for Male, Female, or Nonbinary partners. If a Mii's preference does not align with the target partner, a romantic spark will never occur.

| Requirement | Description | Impact on Romance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Age Group** | Miis must both be Adults or both be Kids. | Prevents incompatible age-gap dating. |
| **Dating Preference** | Must be set to include the target Mii's gender. | Determines if a Mii is "available" to another. |
| **Family Status** | Miis set as "Related" cannot date. | Prevents incestuous relationships on the island. |
| **Relationship Level** | Miis must usually be friends before dating. | Strangers cannot jump directly into romance. |

> **Warning:** If you change a Mii's age to move them into a different bracket (e.g., making a Kid an Adult), it can reset their existing relationships or cause them to break up with current partners.

## How to Influence Mii Interactions

While you cannot directly force a Mii to "Love" another through a simple menu command, you can influence the frequency of their interactions. The more two Miis spend time together, the higher the probability that one will develop a "Love Pondering" (indicated by an orange bubble with a heart).

### Strategic Apartment Placement
One of the most effective long-term strategies is apartment proximity. Miis are more likely to visit their neighbors than Miis living on different floors or far ends of the hallway. If you have a specific pair in mind, move their apartments so they are side-by-side. This increases the chance of them "hanging out" autonomously, which builds the friendship base necessary for romance.

### Shared Items and Hobbies
Giving two Miis the same "Level Up" items can encourage them to interact. When a Mii levels up, you can gift them items like a **Wii U**, **Laptop**, **Skateboard**, or **Disposable Camera**. If two Miis own the same item, you will often find them using those items together in one of their apartments.

| Level Up Item | Social Effect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Travel Ticket** | High chance to boost relationship; Miis may take a friend/partner. |
| **Wii U / Gaming Console** | Encourages Miis to play together in apartments. |
| **Kite / Frisbee** | Often leads to outdoor interactions at the Park or Beach. |
| **Musical Instrument** | Can lead to Miis forming a band or performing together. |

## Forcing the Spark: The "Living the Dream" Method

In the 2026 "Living the Dream" update, players have more direct control over social dynamics. One of the most powerful tools added is the ability to **drag and drop** Miis. If you see a Mii participating in an event (like the Fountain or the Cafe) and they have blue or purple "interaction" sparks near them, you can physically drag another Mii into that space.

Dragging a Mii to interact with another resident can force a meeting between strangers or deepen the bond between friends. Over several days of consistent interaction, the game's "hidden" relationship score will rise, eventually triggering the "Confession" event.

## The Confession: Turning Friends into Sweethearts

The moment you’ve been waiting for arrives when a Mii has an **orange question bubble** in their window. This indicates a "Love Pondering." When you click this bubble, the Mii may say, "I think [Name] needs a sweetheart," or "I have feelings for [Name]. What should I do?"

### Setting Up the Date
If a Mii asks for your advice on a crush, you have several options:
1.  **Encourage them:** Tell them to go for it!
2.  **Choose the location:** You can pick where the confession happens (The Beach, The Roof, The Park, etc.).
3.  **Choose the style:** You can tell them to be "Romantic," "Direct," or even "Cute."

> **Tip:** If the Mii suggests a person you don't want them to date, you can select "Someone else" and choose your preferred Mii from the island roster. However, if their friendship level with your choice is low, the confession is much more likely to fail.

### Handling Confession Failure
Not every confession ends in success. Sometimes the other Mii will say "Sorry," or they might not show up at all. If this happens, your Mii will enter a "Sadness" state. You will need to cheer them up with food, gifts, or mini-games before they are ready to try again. A failed confession doesn't mean the couple is impossible; it just means you need to build their friendship higher before the next attempt.

## Advancing to Marriage and Beyond

Once two Miis become "Sweethearts," the work isn't over. To get them to marry, you must maintain their relationship. You can check their status in the "Relationships" tab—look for phrases like "Greatest Love" or "Let's get married!" 

### Relationship Status Tiers
| Status Level | Meaning | Marriage Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fairly Happy** | The relationship is stable but new. | Low |
| **Totally Happy** | They are very compatible and spend much time together. | Medium |
| **Greatest Love** | This is the highest level of romantic bond. | High |
| **Want to get Married** | A proposal event is imminent. | Guaranteed Proposal |

When a Mii decides they want to propose, a **pink heart bubble** will appear. This triggers a mini-game where you must help the Mii time their proposal correctly during a dinner or a walk. Success leads to a wedding ceremony and the couple moving into the Mii Homes section of the island.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I make two Miis fall in love in Tomodachi Life if they don't know each other?**

A: You must first get them to meet. You can do this by waiting for a mutual friend to introduce them (indicated by an orange bubble) or, in the "Living the Dream" version, by dragging one Mii to the other while they are in a public space. Once they are acquaintances, give them similar items to encourage them to spend time together.

**Q: Why won't my Miis get the option to marry?**

A: Marriage requires a very high relationship status. If they are already "Sweethearts," try giving them Travel Tickets. If they go on a trip together, their relationship level usually gets a massive boost. Also, ensure both Miis are Adults; Kids cannot get married in the game.

**Q: Can Miis have a one-sided crush?**

A: Yes. It is common for one Mii to be in love while the other only views them as a friend. This is called "Unrequited Love." You can still encourage the Mii with the crush to confess, but the success rate is lower than if both Miis have high mutual friendship scores.

**Q: What happens if a Mii is in love with two people at once?**

A: Occasionally, a Mii may develop feelings for a second person while already having a crush. When they ask for your advice, the game will present both options. You can choose which Mii they should pursue, and their relationship with the other "crush" will typically revert to a standard friendship.
