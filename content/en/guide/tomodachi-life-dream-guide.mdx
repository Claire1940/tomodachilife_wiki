## Understanding Interaction Bubbles and Icons

The most frequent way you will interact with your Miis is through the thought bubbles that appear over their heads. Each color and pattern represents a different category of need or event. Ignoring these can lead to missed opportunities for happiness boosts and "fuzzies," which are the primary currency for leveling up your island's fountain.

| Bubble Color/Icon | Meaning | Typical Request |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Yellow Bubble** | General Problems | Hunger, new outfits, room renovations, or pallet house crafts. |
| **Orange Bubble** | Friendship | Asking for an opinion on a potential friend or a conversation topic. |
| **Pink Bubble** | Romance | Developing feelings for another Mii or asking to confess love. |
| **Blue Lines** | Sadness | A Mii is upset because an interaction went poorly or they were rejected. |
| **Colorful Stripes** | Activities | Mini-games, help with being stuck, or placing decorations. |

When a yellow bubble appears, you aren't strictly required to agree to the Mii's request. However, fulfilling these requests—especially by providing items the Mii "Likes" or "Loves"—is the fastest way to earn rewards. If a Mii is sad (indicated by blue lines), feeding them a favorite food is often the most effective way to "turn a frown upside down" and restore their happiness meter.

> **💡 Tip:** Hover over a Mii to see what they are thinking at the bottom of the screen. Sometimes you can encourage them to interact with a Mii they are curious about without a bubble even appearing.

## Tomodachi Life Dream Guide: Maximizing Happiness and Fuzzies

The happiness bar is the lifeblood of your Mii's progression. When this bar fills, the Mii levels up, allowing you to grant them rewards such as new songs, unique gifts, or specialized "appetite quirks." In this **tomodachi life dream guide**, we recommend a specific daily routine to keep your islanders satisfied.

Start your day by checking all active bubbles and icons. Once the immediate needs are met, perform a "feeding sweep" of the entire island. Feeding your Miis not only provides a steady stream of fuzzies for your fountain but also makes it easy to track who has been checked on for the day. 

### The Fountain and Wishes
The water fountain acts as the central hub for island progression. As you collect fuzzies, the fountain levels up, unlocking new hobbies, seasonal objects, and wishes. You do not have to spend your wishes immediately; you can continue to collect fuzzies in the bottle even when it is full, allowing you to redeem multiple rewards in a single session.

## Daily Routines and Marketplace Shifts

The marketplace is one of the most important locations on your island, but its schedule is not explicitly explained to the player. It opens three times a day, with each shift offering a different type of reward.

| Time of Day | Market Shift Focus | Key Reward |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Morning** | Food Items | Rare or discounted ingredients. |
| **Afternoon** | Clothing/Outfits | Seasonal or special attire. |
| **Night** | Mystery Boxes | **$50 Mystery Box** containing three "Gacha" items. |

The night shift is particularly valuable because the mystery boxes often contain high-value treasures. These treasures can be sold at the Pawn Shop (Right Price Shop) for significant profit. Additionally, remember to check the fountain every morning for your daily donation and visit T&C Renovation every Monday for new specialty room interiors.

## Relationship Building and Moving In

Relationships in Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream are dynamic. The relationship tab tracks the progress level between Miis, and these levels fluctuate based on their interactions. One of the best ways to foster new connections is to physically pick up a Mii and hover them over another Mii they haven't met yet. This "nudge" encourages them to introduce themselves, granting you instant fuzzies.

When two Miis become best friends, they may request to move in together. This triggers a specific mini-game where you must choose a topic both Miis enjoy. If they both agree, they will move into a larger house. These houses are highly customizable, featuring up to eight rooms and a common area on the bottom floor.

> **Warning:** Avoid "time traveling" by changing your system clock. Even a small adjustment can lock you out of shop updates and daily events for 24 hours as a penalty.

## Advanced Customization and Mii Creation

Every Mii has one of 16 personality types, which are determined by the sliders you set during creation. These personalities are color-coded, influencing the Mii's starter outfit and the initial style of their apartment. However, the real depth comes from "quirks"—customized ways of walking, eating, and greeting others that unlock as the Mii levels up.

### Personal Wardrobes and Rooms
Items you gift to a Mii stay in their personal wardrobe. This means if you want two Miis to wear the same outfit, you must purchase it twice. While you cannot change the base decor of a room once it is set, you can customize the flooring and walls to give it a unique flair.

### Mii Inspiration and Sharing
If you are looking for new islanders, the [Tomodachi Share website](https://www.nintendo.com/jp/3ds/ec6j/index.html) is an excellent resource for inspiration. While the Miitopia demo allows for Mii sharing, many of those designs use excessive "face paint" that does not translate well into the Tomodachi Life engine. For the best results, manually replicate Miis using basic features to ensure they look correct in-game.

## Economic Strategies: Making Money Fast

To maintain a high-end island, you will need a steady flow of cash. Beyond the daily fountain donations, selling items is your primary source of income. Treasures can be acquired through various means:
*   Winning mini-games (Medium and Large boxes are generally the most consistent).
*   Watching Mii dreams while they sleep.
*   Sending Miis on trips using "Travel Tickets."
*   Purchasing Mystery Boxes during the night shift at the marketplace.

You can also earn small amounts of money and fuzzies by interacting with your Miis directly, such as rubbing their heads or playing games with them. While these individual amounts are small, they add up quickly when you have a full roster of islanders. As highlighted in this **tomodachi life dream guide**, the more Miis you have, the faster your total wealth and fountain level will grow.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get my Miis to move in together?**

A: Miis must first become best friends. Once their relationship level is high enough, one will eventually ask to move in with the other. You will need to successfully complete a "shared interest" cutscene where both Miis agree on a topic they like.

**Q: What is the best way to earn fuzzies according to this tomodachi life dream guide?**

A: The most consistent way to earn fuzzies is by fulfilling yellow and orange bubble requests. Additionally, introducing Miis to one another and feeding them their favorite foods will provide significant happiness boosts and fountain experience.

**Q: Can I change a Mii's personality after they are created?**

A: You cannot directly change the 16-type personality classification, but you can heavily influence their behavior by assigning different quirks and hobbies as they level up. You can also edit their nicknames and pronouns at any time via the Mii menu (Y button).

**Q: Why are my shops not updating with new items?**

A: This usually happens if you have changed your system's internal clock. The game detects time manipulation and will lock shop inventories for 24 to 48 hours. It is best to set your time once and leave it alone to avoid this penalty.
