## What Hair Color Spray Actually Does in Tomodachi Life

Hair color spray is a consumable gift item that lets a Mii apply a temporary color change to their visible hair-related features. In normal cases, this means head hair. In special cases, the color can affect other visible “hair” elements tied to the Mii’s design, such as brows or facial hair.

| Feature | How spray affects it | What to expect |
|---|---|---|
| **Head hair** | Direct recolor | **Most obvious result** on medium/long hairstyles |
| **Eyebrows** | Can reflect selected tone | More noticeable on dark, thick eyebrow styles |
| **Mustache/beard** | Often follows hair tone logic | Big visual change on facial-hair Miis |
| **Bald or near-bald styles** | Limited or indirect effect | Color shift may look minor or move to facial features |

> **Tip:** Use hair color spray when a Mii asks for a “new look” or “new hair color.” You often get better satisfaction value than random clothing gifts.

Because cosmetic requests are mood-driven, the spray is part styling tool and part relationship management tool. If you rotate through residents frequently, this item helps keep request chains moving.

## Step-by-Step: How to Use Hair Color Spray Efficiently

Once you have spray in inventory, usage is straightforward—but results improve when you plan the order of actions.

1. Open the Mii apartment and select the resident.

3. Confirm the color request prompt.  
4. Preview the result and finalize.

| Step | Common mistake | Better approach |

### Color planning shortcut

Use this rule before confirming:
- **High contrast** (black to blonde, brown to blue): bold/funny look
- **Low contrast** (brown to dark brown): subtle realism
- **Novel colors** (pink, green-ish tones, bright yellow): best for comedy islands or themed casts

## Bald Miis, Facial Hair, and Other Edge Cases

A major community question in 2026 is still: “Why did my bald Mii ask for hair color spray?”  
Answer: the request system and appearance system are not perfectly literal. A bald-style Mii can still trigger hair-related requests because the game classifies certain facial features as color-compatible with hair logic.

### What to expect with bald residents

| Mii type | Likely visual outcome after spray | Recommended use |
|---|---|---|
| **Fully bald, no facial hair** | Very small visible change | Use only if you want request completion |
| **Bald + thick eyebrows** | Brows can shift noticeably | Good for comedic expression |
| **Bald + mustache/beard** | Facial hair color may pop | Great for character redesigns |
| **Hat-heavy Mii** | Change hidden by accessory | Check without hat before judging |

This is why many players think the item “failed.” In reality, the color was applied to visible hair-linked features that are easy to miss under hats or minimal brows.

### When to skip spray on bald Miis

- You only have 1-2 spray items left  
- You are saving cosmetics for relationship events/screenshots  
- The Mii has no visible features likely to show the color well

If you just need to solve their request and maintain happiness flow, go ahead and use it. If you are optimizing for visuals, save it for more visible targets.

## FAQ

**Q: Is tomodachi life hair color spray permanent?**

A: In normal play, it behaves like a temporary cosmetic change rather than a fully locked identity setting. Future requests, styling changes, and appearance updates can alter how that Mii looks again.

**Q: Why does tomodachi life hair color spray seem useless on bald Miis?**

A: Bald styles reduce visible head-hair surface area. The color may only show on eyebrows or facial hair, so the effect can look minor unless those features are prominent.

**Q: What is the best color choice for readability in apartment scenes?**

A: Pick high-contrast colors relative to skin tone and outfit. Medium-dark blues, warm blondes, and strong browns usually read clearly in indoor lighting.

**Q: Should I spend spray for request completion or save it for style?**

A: Early game: prioritize request completion to keep progression smooth. Mid/late game: reserve spray for themed groups, relationship screenshots, or standout characters.
