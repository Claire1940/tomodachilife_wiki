The **tomodachi life concert hall** serves as the primary creative hub for your islanders to express their musical talents. Unlike other locations that appear based on population or item collection, the Concert Hall is directly tied to individual Mii progression. To access this venue, you must wait until a Mii levels up. Upon leveling up, you are presented with several gift options; selecting a **Song** will immediately unlock the Concert Hall location on your island map.

Once unlocked, the Concert Hall allows Miis to perform solo or in groups. The songs provided are entirely new for this iteration of the franchise, expanding significantly on the mechanics found in the original *Tomodachi Collection*. <Section icon={Sparkles} title="Song Genres and Regional Variations">

The variety of music in the **tomodachi life concert hall** depends heavily on which version of the game you are playing. While the core experience remains consistent, Nintendo tailored the tracklist to suit different cultural markets. For instance, the Japanese version features the traditional **Enka** genre, while North American and PAL versions replaced this with **Ballad**, **Techno**, and **Musical**.

Understanding these genres is crucial for players looking to maximize their island's entertainment value. Each genre features unique stage choreography and instrumental backing. The **Metal** genre, for example, is heavily influenced by classic bands like Metallica and Judas Priest, featuring high-energy animations and pyrotechnics.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Mastering Group Performances">

Forming a band or a pop group in the **tomodachi life concert hall** requires careful planning. You cannot simply throw any Miis together; they must share a musical repertoire. The game allows for a maximum of 50 custom groups and 50 unique group songs to be saved, providing plenty of room for creative experimentation.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Distribute Songs">
    Gift the exact same song genre to every Mii you want in the group. If one Mii lacks the 'Techno' song, they cannot join the 'Techno' group.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assemble the Lineup">
    Enter the Concert Hall and select 'Group.' Choose the song and then select 2 to 7 Miis from your eligible islanders.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Assign Positions">
    The order in which you select Miis determines their stage position. You can swap positions by clicking the clothes icon in the top-left corner and sliding the Mii portraits.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Customize the Show">
    Change the group name and edit the lyrics to fit your band's persona. You can also change their outfits to ensure a cohesive look.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Performance Limits">
The **Rap** group performance is limited to only two Miis singing, while **Techno** features three. If you fill a group with seven Miis for these genres, the remaining islanders will act as background dancers or instrumentalists.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="Lyric Customization and Technical Limits">

One of the most engaging aspects of the **tomodachi life concert hall** is the ability to rewrite every song. Whether you want your Miis to sing about island drama or create a parody of a real-world hit, the lyric editor is surprisingly robust. However, there are technical boundaries that players must respect to avoid game stability issues.

In the Japanese and Korean versions, entering a Mii's name or a specific food item into the lyrics will cause that item's icon or the Mii's face to appear on the background screens during the performance. This visual flair adds a layer of personalization that makes every concert feel unique to your island's history.

| Constraint | Limit / Detail | Consequence of Breach |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Character Count** | Standard text box limit | Lyrics will lag or cut off |
| **Special Characters** | Symbols like "h°" or excessive ü | Potential game crash |
| **Profanity Filter** | Built-in Nintendo censor | Blocked words (bypassed by spaces) |
| **Empty Lyrics** | No text entered | Miis sing "duh-duh-duh" gibberish |
| **Lyric Length** | Excessively long lines | Performance ends with error message |

<Callout type="tip" title="Lyric Strategy">
If you want to bypass the profanity filter for creative reasons, using symbols or euphemisms is common. However, be aware that excessively long lyrics can trigger the "The lyrics you entered could not be sung properly" error, forcing the performance to end prematurely.
</Callout>

</Section>
