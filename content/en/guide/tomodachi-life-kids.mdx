<Section icon={Map} title="Unlocking and Requesting Babies">

The journey of **tomodachi life kids** begins long before the stork arrives. To witness the miracle of life on your island, you must first ensure that your island settings allow for it. By default, the baby option is enabled, but it is always wise to double-check your configuration if your married couples seem disinterested in expanding their families.

To manage this, head to the **Town Hall** and look for the Baby Option. In the *Living the Dream* version, this may also be found within the general options menu. If this is toggled off, no new requests will occur, though existing children will remain on the island. Once enabled, the likelihood of a request is heavily influenced by the relationship status of your married Miis.

| Relationship Status | Likelihood of Baby Request | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Soul Mates** | Extremely High | Check for love bubbles frequently |
| **Greatest Happiness** | High | Give them travel tickets to boost bonds |
| **Very Happy** | Moderate | Interact with them daily |
| **Happy** | Low | Improve relationship with gifts |
| **Getting Along** | Very Low | Focus on relationship repair |

When a Mii is ready to have a baby, a pink love icon will appear over their apartment or house. Upon visiting, they will express that they are "thinking about having a baby." As the island's look-alike, you have the power to encourage or discourage this. Encouraging them sets the due date for approximately 24 hours later (or up to 2.5 days in *Living the Dream*).

<Callout type="tip" title="Relationship Boost">
If you want to speed up the process for **tomodachi life kids**, use a Travel Ticket on a married couple. The shared vacation significantly boosts their relationship level, making a baby request much more likely to trigger within the next few play sessions.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Childbirth and Customization Mechanics">

When the due date arrives, you will receive a phone call from one of the parents while overlooking the island. This triggers a special cutscene where the parents introduce their newborn. This is a critical moment for the player, as it is the only time you can influence the baby's fundamental traits before they reach adulthood.

During the birth event, you will be asked three primary questions that define the child's identity:

1.  **Gender Preference**: You can choose a boy, a girl, or let the game decide randomly.
2.  **Appearance**: The game generates a face based on a hybrid of both parents' features. You can accept this or enter the Mii Maker to fine-tune their look.
3.  **Personality**: You can choose to have the baby inherit a parent's personality, generate a random one, or manually set the sliders yourself.

<Callout type="warning" title="Irreversible Choices">
In the *Living the Dream* edition, you cannot edit the baby's face immediately upon birth. You must wait for the initial cutscene to conclude before any modifications can be made. Choose your initial settings carefully!
</Callout>

The baby's personality and gender also determine the color of their initial clothing. This color scheme persists through most of their childhood stages, providing a visual cue of their temperament.

| Personality Type | Male Clothing Color | Female Clothing Color |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Outgoing** | Yellow | Yellow |
| **Easygoing** | Green | Red |
| **Independent** | Blue | Purple |
| **Confident** | Orange | Pink |

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The 5 Stages of Growth">

The development of **tomodachi life kids** is a rapid process, spanning roughly six real-time days. Each day marks a significant milestone in the child's physical and social development. If you fail to log in for a day, the child may skip a stage entirely, so daily check-ins are essential for those who want to experience every moment.

<Table>
| Day | Growth Stage | Physical Changes | Key Behaviors |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Day 1** | **Newborn** | Stays in bassinet, wrapped in blanket | Crying, sleeping, being held |
| **Day 2** | **Crawler** | Moves on hands and knees | Exploring the floor, babbling |
| **Day 3** | **Stander** | Attempts to stand, hair begins to grow | Falling over, sitting up properly |
| **Day 4** | **Toddler** | Walking (unsteady), toddler proportions | Playing with toys, less crying |
| **Day 5** | **Child** | Full hair, final head shape | Speaking clearly, running |
| **Day 6** | **Adult** | Reaches full Mii height | Ready to move out or travel |
</Table>

During these stages, the parents' house becomes the hub of activity. You can visit them to see the family interacting. Interestingly, while a couple is raising a child, they are immune to divorce or major fights, ensuring a stable environment for the baby's growth.

<Callout type="success" title="Growth Secret">
If you notice a stage 5 child is no longer making baby noises and is instead speaking in a high-pitched version of their parents' voices, they are mere hours away from their final growth spurt. Prepare your apartment space now!
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Babysitting Mini-Games and Interaction">

Raising **tomodachi life kids** is a hands-on experience. Parents will frequently call the player for help when the baby is inconsolable. Successfully calming a baby rewards you with a gift, usually a food item or a common treasure. The mechanics of these mini-games change as the child grows.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stage 1: Rocking">
    For newborns, you must use the 3DS/2DS gyro controls. Hold the console flat and gently tilt it back and forth. Look for sparkles and flowers around the baby's head as a sign of success. Avoid jerky movements, or the parents will scold you!
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Stages 2-3: Soothing">
    As the baby becomes more aware, you must tickle their stomach, pat their head, or play peekaboo. Each baby has randomized preferences. One might love being tickled but hate peekaboo. Watch for the black spiral icon, which indicates a mistake.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Stages 4-5: Playing">
    Once the child is a toddler, the "Babysit" button changes to a play option. You can spin the child in circles using the touch screen. While they won't cry for help at this stage, playing with them increases the family's overall happiness.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Nighttime Crying">
If you open the game after 9:00 PM and hear crying on the title screen, it means a baby is awake. Visiting the house will show the parents in their pajamas, exhausted. Manually babysitting and successfully putting the baby to sleep allows the whole family to rest.
</Callout>

| Interaction | Expected Reaction | Best Stage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tickling** | Laughter / Farting | Stage 2-3 |
| **Peekaboo** | Surprise / Giggling | Stage 3 |
| **Patting** | Calmness / Sleepiness | Stage 1-2 |
| **Spinning** | Dizziness / Joy | Stage 4-5 |

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="The Final Decision: Stay or Leave">

On the sixth day, the parents will call you one last time. The child has reached adulthood and is ready to face the world. This is a pivotal moment for your island's demographics, as the choice you make is permanent.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Move into Apartments">
    - The child becomes a **permanent resident**.
    - They require an empty room in the Mii Apartments.
    - You can fully customize their profile and catchphrase.
    - They interact with other Miis and can eventually marry.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Send on a Journey">
    - The child becomes a **Traveler**.
    - They depart via the Port to visit other players' islands.
    - They send back letters and photos of their travels.
    - They occasionally return to visit their parents for a day.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

If your apartments are full, you will be forced to either delete an existing Mii or send the child traveling. Similarly, if your Port is overcrowded with travelers, the child cannot leave until space is cleared.

<Callout type="warning" title="Population Cap">
Always keep 1-2 apartment rooms empty if you are expecting **tomodachi life kids**. If a child grows up and there is no room, you'll miss the opportunity to keep them as a resident, which is often the preferred choice for legacy islands.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Pro Tips and Trivia for Mii Parents">

Mastering the mechanics of **tomodachi life kids** requires knowing the hidden details that the game doesn't explicitly tell you. From genetic inheritance to regional differences, there is much to discover.

- **Genetic Inheritance**: Hair color and facial features are usually a 50/50 split between parents, but hair styles can be entirely random or follow "trait groups." For example, if a mother has her hair tied up, daughters are very likely to develop ponytails or braids.
- **The Blonde Eyebrow Mystery**: Regardless of hair color, almost all babies have blonde eyebrows until Stage 5. This is a nod to real-life biology where infant eyebrows are often too fine to see clearly.
- **Regional Differences**: In North American and European versions, the "family bath" scene was removed. Instead, Miis are shown taking individual showers to comply with Western ratings.
- **Inherited Quirks**: In the *Living the Dream* mod, children can inherit a parent's "Little Quirk" or specific catchphrases, adding a deeper layer of personality inheritance.
