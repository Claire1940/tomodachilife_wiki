### Duplicate Buildings: Aesthetic vs. Utility

A common question among players is the purpose of purchasing duplicate buildings. While it might seem like a way to get more services or special items, the reality is simpler.

| Feature             | Single Building | Duplicate Building | Primary Purpose     |
|:--------------------|:----------------|:-------------------|:--------------------|
| **Core Functionality** | Fully functional | No additional function | Essential services |
| **Special Events**  | Yes             | No additional events | Mii interaction     |
| **Item Stock**      | Unique items    | No additional stock | Goods & services    |
| **Decoration**      | Yes             | Yes                | Aesthetic appeal    |
| **Cost**            | Standard        | Standard           | Resource expenditure |

Duplicate buildings in Tomodachi Life are purely for **decorative purposes**. If you want two restaurants or multiple clothing stores for aesthetic reasons, you can buy them. However, they won't provide extra specials, more items, or increased functionality. One of each functional building is sufficient for gameplay.

### Night Market Strategy

The Night Market's mystery bag is a daily chance for valuable items.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Save Before Bag">
    Save your game immediately before opening the Night Market mystery bag.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Inspect Contents">
    Open the bag and review the items. Do not exit the item display screen.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Reroll (If Needed)">
    If you dislike the items, close the game application without saving. Reload to try again.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Accept or Wait">
    If satisfied, save the game. If not, you can only reroll once per day; otherwise, wait until the next day.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Mii Quirks and Their Impact

Certain Mii quirks can significantly alter their behavior, which can be beneficial for specific playstyles or for simply having more active Miis.

| Quirk Type    | Effect                                     | Benefit                                        |
|:--------------|:-------------------------------------------|:-----------------------------------------------|
| **Night Owl** | Miis sleep during the day, active at night | Ideal for players who play primarily at night  |
| **Big Eater** | Miis can consume more food                 | Faster level-ups, more friendship from feeding |
| Light Eater   | Miis consume less food                     | Less effective for rapid leveling              |

You unlock more quirks by upgrading your island's fountain and granting wishes. Prioritize the Night Owl if you're a night player, and Big Eater for faster Mii progression.

### Troubleshooting Sleeping Miis

Sometimes, you might want to interact with a sleeping Mii. While they might resist, there's a trick to wake them up.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Lift Mii from Bed">
    Pick up the sleeping Mii from their bed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Take Outside">
    Drag them outside their apartment. They might try to go back to sleep.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Force Interaction">
    If they go back inside, pick them up again and force them to interact with an item outside (e.g., a lamp post).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Observe Wake-Up">
    They should then wake up, change into their normal clothes, and become active on the island.
  </Step>
</Steps>
