<Section icon={Utensils} title="Food Categories and Fullness Mechanics">

To effectively manage your islanders, you must understand the four main categories of **tomodachi life favorite foods**. Each category fills the Mii's hunger meter differently. Overfeeding a Mii will result in them refusing to eat more, while underfeeding prevents them from leveling up through food-based happiness.

| Category | Item Count (US) | Fullness Value | Best Use Case |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **Main Dishes** | 43 Items | High | Use when the hunger meter is nearly empty |
| **Side Dishes** | 83 Items | Medium | General feeding and testing for favorites |
| **Desserts** | 81 Items | Low-Medium | Topping off the hunger bar for a level-up |
| **Beverages** | 24 Items | Low | Use when the Mii is almost full but needs a boost |

<Callout type="warning" title="Fullness Warning">
Miis will refuse to eat once their stomach meter is full. If you are trying to find a Mii's favorite food, start with beverages and desserts, as you can test more items before they become too full to continue.
</Callout>

In addition to standard foods, there are "Special Foods" and "Trash Foods" that operate outside the normal preference system.

| Food Type | Origin | Effect |
|:--- |:--- |:--- |
| **Special Foods** | Events/Gifts | Guaranteed high happiness; never a personal favorite/worst |
| **Trash Food** | Dreams/Frying Pan | Guaranteed dislike; includes Moldy Bread and Spoiled Milk |
| **Regional Items** | StreetPass/Import | Unique foods from other islands or real-world regions |

<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: How to Discover Favorite Foods">

Finding the specific **tomodachi life favorite foods** for your favorite Mii can be a long process of trial and error. Follow these steps to systematically unlock their preferences and fill out their profile.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Stock Your Inventory">
    Visit the Food Mart daily and buy one of every available item. Aim to have at least 20-30 different items in your inventory before starting a testing session.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check the Hunger Meter">
    Ensure your Mii is hungry. A Mii with an empty stomach can usually taste 3-5 items before becoming full.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Test by Category">
    Start with items you have in large quantities. Feed the Mii one item at a time and observe the reaction. If they do a dance or launch into space, you've found a favorite!
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Document the Results">
    The game automatically records Favorites and Hated foods in the Mii's profile (accessible via the Apartment or Town Hall). Check this list to see which slots are still empty.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Use the Frying Pan">
    If you are struggling to find new items, give a Mii a Frying Pan (a level-up gift). They will cook a random dish, which might be a new addition to your collection.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Strategy">
If you find a Mii's **Super All-Time Favorite**, save it! Don't feed it to them casually. Keep a small stock of that specific item to use only when that Mii is sick or extremely sad, as it is the most powerful recovery tool in the game.
</Callout>

</Section>
