## The Foundation: From Friends to Sweethearts

Before you can even think about wedding bells, your Miis must establish a romantic foundation. Marriage in Tomodachi Life is a tiered progression system. You cannot force two Miis to marry if they haven't first successfully navigated the "Dating" phase.

To begin, two Miis must become friends. Once they have a solid friendship, one Mii will eventually develop feelings for the other. This is indicated by a pink "heart" thought bubble appearing in their apartment window. When you see this, the Mii will ask for your advice on confessing their love. If the confession is successful, they officially become a couple.

### Relationship Status Stages

| Stage | Requirement | Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Friends** | Initial interaction and successful introduction. | Miis visit each other's apartments frequently. |
| **Sweethearts** | Successful confession of love. | Miis go on dates and show up in each other's status screens. |
| **Engaged** | Successful completion of the Proposal Mini-game. | The wedding ceremony is triggered immediately. |
| **Married** | Completion of the wedding and honeymoon. | Miis move into the Mii Homes area together. |

> **💡 Tip:** If a Mii is nervous about confessing, suggest they use a "Romantic" approach or give them a gift like a Mirror to boost their confidence before they head out.

## Strengthening the Bond

Once your Miis are dating, you need to raise their relationship level. You can check this by looking at the Mii's profile and viewing their relationship list. Phrases like "Greatest Love" or "Let's get married!" indicate that a proposal is imminent. If the relationship is merely "Happy" or "Getting along," you have more work to do.

To strengthen the bond quickly, encourage frequent interaction. If one Mii is feeling lonely or bored, try to "drag" the other Mii into the room (figuratively, by answering their prompts to hang out). Providing specific items can also accelerate the process. 

**Travel Tickets** are the most effective tool in your arsenal. Giving a Travel Ticket to a Mii who is in a relationship will often result in them taking their partner on a romantic vacation. These trips generate high-quality memories, romantic photos, and a massive boost to their relationship score.

## The Proposal: Mastering the Mini-Game

When a Mii is ready to pop the question, a pink heart thought bubble will appear, but this time they will specifically mention wanting to marry their partner. This triggers the proposal event. Unlike the confession of love, which is largely cinematic, the proposal requires active player participation.

### How the Proposal Mini-Game Works

The mini-game puts you in the role of the "inner voice" or "timing coach" for the Mii. You will see a scene, such as a restaurant or a park, where the couple is sitting together. Your goal is to tap the "Now!" button (or press A) when the Mii is thinking about their partner.

1.  **Watch the Thought Bubbles:** Bubbles will float above the Mii's head showing various items or other Miis.
2.  **Wait for the Partner's Face:** You must wait until the bubble shows the face of the Mii's partner.
3.  **Timing is Everything:** Tap the heart icon exactly when the partner's face is visible.
4.  **Manage Your Lives:** You typically have three attempts. If you mistime the tap three times, the Mii loses their nerve, and the proposal fails.

| Element | Action | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Partner's Face** | Tap "Now!" immediately. | Progresses the proposal meter. |
| **Distractions (Food, Hobbies)** | Do not tap. | No penalty, but wastes time. |
| **Wrong Mii Face** | Do not tap. | Tapping here counts as a fail. |
| **Empty Bubble** | Do not tap. | Tapping here counts as a fail. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful not to get overwhelmed if the bubbles start moving faster. It is better to skip a cycle and wait for a clear shot than to rush and lose a life.

## Requirements for a Successful Marriage

Even if you are following every step of **tomodachi life how to get married**, there are hard requirements built into the game's logic that can prevent the event from triggering.

*   **Age Compatibility:** Both Miis must be in the same age bracket. In Tomodachi Life, Miis are classified as either "Child" or "Adult" based on the birth date you entered. Two adults can marry, and two children can marry (using an "Age-O-Matic" to temporarily become adults for the ceremony), but an Adult and a Child cannot marry.
*   **Relationship Level:** The relationship status should ideally be at least "Very Happy" or higher. While it is possible at lower levels, the "marriage" thought bubble is much rarer.
*   **Apartment Space:** Ensure your island isn't at maximum capacity, though the game usually prioritizes the move to Mii Homes regardless.

## Life After the Wedding

Once the proposal is accepted, the wedding ceremony begins immediately. You’ll see a montage of the Miis in their wedding attire, followed by a honeymoon sequence at a random international location. Upon their return, several things change:

1.  **Mii Homes:** A new area of the island called "Mii Homes" becomes active. The married couple will move into a house here.
2.  **Dual Residency:** The Miis will still keep their individual apartments in the main building, but they will spend most of their time (especially at night) in their shared home.
3.  **Babies:** Marriage is the prerequisite for the "Baby" mechanic. After some time, a married couple may ask you if they should have a child. 

### Post-Marriage Benefits

| Benefit | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **New Items** | You receive a special "Honeymoon Gift" (treasures) that can be sold for high amounts of silver/gold. |
| **Stability** | Married Miis are less likely to have "breakup" fights, though they still require occasional conflict resolution. |
| **Island Growth** | Children born to married Miis can eventually become permanent residents, expanding your population. |

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

If you are struggling with **tomodachi life how to get married**, it usually comes down to the RNG (Random Number Generation) of the thought bubbles.

**Q: My Miis have been dating for weeks but won't propose. What's wrong?**
Check their compatibility at the Compatibility Tester. While a low score doesn't make marriage impossible, it makes the "marriage" thought bubble appear much less frequently. Try giving them more gifts that they both like to increase their shared happiness.

**Q: Can I stop a divorce?**
Yes. If you see a purple "sad" bubble, the Miis might be considering a breakup or divorce. Step in immediately to resolve the fight. Giving them a Travel Ticket during this phase can sometimes save the marriage.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get my Miis to get married faster in Tomodachi Life?**

A: To speed up the process, give both Miis Travel Tickets to boost their relationship status to the maximum level. Also, ensure you respond to every "problem" bubble they have, as resolving small issues increases their overall happiness and the likelihood of a proposal bubble appearing.

**Q: Can Miis get married if they are different ages?**

A: No, an adult Mii and a child Mii cannot get married. You must use an "Age-O-Matic" item on the child Mii to turn them into an adult if you want them to marry another adult resident.

**Q: What happens if I fail the proposal mini-game?**

A: If you fail, the Mii will be heartbroken and their relationship level will drop slightly. You will need to cheer them up and wait for the proposal thought bubble to appear again, which may take several days or even weeks.

**Q: How many times can a Mii get married?**

A: A Mii can get married multiple times, but they must be divorced or widowed first. There is no limit to how many times a Mii can find love on your island, provided their previous relationships have officially ended.
