## The Logic Behind Mii Food Preferences

For years, players believed that food preferences were entirely random or determined at the moment of feeding. However, deep dives into the game's source code by community groups like Tomodachi Enterprises have revealed a much more sophisticated reality. Rather than a simple random number generator, the game uses an algorithmic approach to determine likability based on hidden "attribute flags" attached to every food item.

When you offer a Mii a piece of **tomodachi life custom food** or a standard inventory item, the game checks that item's attributes against the Mii's internal preference profile. This profile isn't just a list of liked items; it is a set of weights assigned to different flavors, origins, and textures.

| Attribute Category | Examples of Flags | Impact on Preference |
|---|---|---|
| **Flavor Profile** | Sweet, Salty, Sour, Spicy | Primary driver of initial reaction. |
| **Temperature** | Hot, Cold | Can override flavor if a Mii has a "Cold" preference. |
| **Origin** | Japanese, Western, Chinese, American | Influences Miis based on their internal "cultural" weight. |
| **Food Group** | Meat, Fruit, Vegetable, Bread, Rice | Determines long-term nutritional satisfaction. |
| **Quality** | Luxury, Awful, Oily | Higher "Luxury" flags generally yield better reactions. |

> **💡 Tip:** If a Mii specifically requests a type of food (e.g., "I want something spicy"), feeding them an item that matches that attribute will almost always result in a positive reaction, regardless of their permanent preference for that specific item.

## Understanding the "Taste Values" (A, B, C, D)

Beyond the basic attributes like "sweet" or "spicy," the game utilizes four mysterious internal values labeled A, B, C, and D. These values act as a secondary layer of controlled randomness. This explains why two Miis who both enjoy "Sweet" and "Western" foods might still have different reactions to a Slice of Cake.

These values likely represent:
1.  **Texture Sensitivity:** How the Mii reacts to crunchy vs. soft foods.
2.  **Aroma Weight:** A hidden modifier that can boost or lower the base score.
3.  **Satiety Factor:** How much the food fills the Mii’s hunger bar relative to its likability.
4.  **Complexity Modifier:** A value that determines if the Mii prefers simple ingredients or complex dishes.

### How Custom Food Attributes are Assigned
When creating a custom item, modders must toggle specific boolean (true/false) flags. If you create a "Custom Bubble Tea," you would set the following:
*   **Sweet:** True
*   **Cold:** True
*   **Confectionary:** True
*   **Drink:** True

If these flags are not set correctly, the Mii might react to the Bubble Tea as if it were a hot bowl of ramen, leading to immersion-breaking animations.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I actually cook tomadachi life custom food in the game?**

A: In the unmodded version of the game, you cannot "cook" or combine ingredients to create new items. You can only obtain food through the Food Mart, donations, or morning markets. To get truly custom items, you must use homebrew software and mods developed by the community.

**Q: Why did my Mii change their mind about a food they used to like?**

A: While permanent preferences (like All-Time Favorites) do not change, a Mii's immediate reaction can be influenced by their hunger level. If a Mii is "starving," they may show a more positive reaction to a food they usually find mediocre. However, their official ranking in the "Collection" menu will remain consistent with their true preference.

**Q: Does the Mii's personality affect their favorite food?**

A: There is no definitive evidence in the source code that personality types (like "Easygoing" or "Outgoing") directly lock certain food preferences. However, certain personality types may request food more frequently, giving you more opportunities to discover their **tomodachi life custom food** preferences through requests.

**Q: How do I get more rare food items?**

A: Rare items like "Truffles" or "Caviar" usually appear in the Food Mart at a low spawn rate. You can also obtain them by winning mini-games against Miis or by checking the "Special Foods" section if you have connected with other players via StreetPass (though this is rare in 2026).
