## Essential Early-Game Facilities

The initial phase of the game is designed to introduce you to the core mechanics of Mii care. Most early buildings are tied directly to the number of Miis you have created and their basic needs, such as hunger and clothing.

| Building Name | Unlock Requirement | Primary Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fresh Kingdom** | Complete the initial tutorial | Purchase food and snacks for Miis |
| **Wear and Wear** | Create 3 Miis + solve a clothing request | Buy outfits and daily clothing specials |
| **TNC Renault** | Create 4 Miis + establish 2 friendships | Purchase and change room interiors |
| **Quick Build** | Create 5 Miis | Design and place interactable island parts |

The **Fresh Kingdom** is your most vital resource, as feeding your Miis is the primary way to gain experience and level them up. Shortly after, **Wear and Wear** opens up, providing the aesthetic customization that the series is known for. It is important to note that **TNC Renault** requires a bit of social engineering; you must encourage your Miis to interact until at least two of them become friends before this interior shop becomes available.

> **💡 Tip:** To foster friendships quickly, look for orange bubbles in Mii windows. These often indicate a desire to meet someone new or resolve a conflict.

## Creative Workshops and Customization

One of the standout features of the "Living the Dream" edition is the expanded focus on player creativity. The **tomodachi life living the dream all buildings** list includes several facilities dedicated to designing your own items and treasures.

### The Pallet House and My Treasures
The **Pallet House Workshop** is unlocked once you have seven Miis living on your island. This is a game-changer for players who enjoy DIY elements, as it allows for the design of custom clothing, interiors, and even pets. 

Immediately after you create your first item in the Pallet House, the **My Treasures** facility will unlock. This shop acts as a catalog for your creations, allowing you to buy copies of your custom items to gift to other residents. This synergy between the two buildings makes the seven-Mii milestone one of the most important in the game.

### Quick Build and Island Decoration
Unlocked at five Miis, **Quick Build** allows you to add flair to the island's exterior. Unlike the 3DS original, this version offers more interactable objects that Miis can actually use. You can even find seasonal items here, provided you have been diligent with your donations at the Wishing Fountain.

## Social and Leisure Destinations

As your population grows, your Miis will need places to hang out and fall in love. These buildings often have higher requirements or depend on specific social outcomes rather than just raw Mii counts.

### Tomoria Restaurant
Unlocked at eight Miis, the **Tomoria Restaurant** is a social hub. While it doesn't sell items directly like the Fresh Kingdom, it provides a backdrop for unique cutscenes. You might find two Miis sharing a meal or a group of friends chatting at a booth. Fans of the original 3DS game will recognize the background music here—it’s a nostalgic remix of the Concert Hall theme.

### The Ferris Wheel and Romance
The **Ferris Wheel** is the pinnacle of island romance. To unlock it, a Mii must successfully confess their love to another. However, there is some community debate regarding island size; if the confession happens very early, you may need to expand your island by adding more Miis before the Ferris Wheel physically appears. This location is a prime spot for marriage proposals later in the game.

### Phototomo
If you enjoy the photography aspect of the game, **Phototomo** is essential. It requires **10 different friendships** to be established on the island. This is one of the more "optional" buildings, as it doesn't impact the economy or Mii survival, but it offers extensive tools for taking high-quality portraits of your residents.

## Economy and Rare Items

Managing your island's finances is key to buying expensive interiors and rare gifts. Two specific buildings handle the buying and selling of non-standard items.

| Building Name | Unlock Requirement | Best Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Right Price Pawn Shop** | Obtain a treasure from a Mii | Sell unwanted treasures for extra gold |
| **Marketplace** | Create 8 Miis | Buy rare items at specific times of day |

The **Right Price Pawn Shop** is unlocked the moment you receive a treasure. You can get treasures by winning minigames (look for the green bubbles) or by checking in on Mii dreams at night. Even a "pity prize" like a box of tissues counts as a treasure for the purposes of unlocking this shop.

The **Marketplace** operates on a real-time clock. It features Morning, Afternoon, and Night markets, each offering different rare goods. This is the best place to find items that aren't available in the standard rotating stock of the Fresh Kingdom or Wear and Wear.

## Advanced Unlocks: The Wishing Fountain

The **Wishing Fountain** is perhaps the most unique facility in this version. It unlocks automatically when you level up a Mii for the first time. It serves two main purposes:
1.  **Donations:** Every day, Miis will leave small amounts of money here based on their happiness levels.
2.  **Wishes:** As you collect "Warm Fuzzies" (a secondary currency gained from making Miis happy), you can grant wishes.

Wishes are the primary way to unlock "sub-content" within other buildings. For example, you can use the fountain to unlock new room types for the Pallet House or special seasonal decorations for Quick Build. It acts as the progression hub for the mid-to-late game.

> **Warning:** Don't forget to collect your daily donations! The fountain has a cap on how much it can hold, and once it's full, you'll lose out on potential daily income.

## Housing and Residential Progression

The way your Miis live changes as the game progresses. While every Mii starts in a **Single House**, their living arrangements can evolve based on their relationships.

*   **Single Houses:** Automatically created for every Mii you add to the island.
*   **Shared Mii House:** This unlocks when two Miis decide to move in together. This can happen between close friends or as a result of marriage. A single shared house can hold up to eight Miis, making it an efficient way to manage a large population.
*   **Mii News Station:** Unlocked at seven Miis. While not a "house," it tracks the lives of all residents. Once fully unlocked as a building, you can view a library of past broadcasts, allowing you to relive the biggest events in your island's history.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get treasures to unlock the Pawn Shop?**

A: The easiest way is to play minigames with your Miis when they have a green bubble. You can also find treasures by tapping on Miis while they are dreaming at night.

**Q: Why hasn't the Ferris Wheel unlocked after a confession?**

A: In **tomodachi life living the dream all buildings** progression, some facilities require a certain island size. If your population is low, try adding more Miis to trigger the island expansion, which should allow the Ferris Wheel to appear.

**Q: What is the maximum number of Miis for all unlocks?**

A: To unlock the majority of the functional buildings, you need at least 10 Miis and 10 established friendships. However, the Shared Mii House and Ferris Wheel are dependent on social actions rather than just the Mii count.

**Q: Can I lose buildings once they are unlocked?**

A: No, once a facility is unlocked on your island, it remains there permanently, even if the number of Miis or friendships drops (though it is rare for friendships to decrease significantly).
