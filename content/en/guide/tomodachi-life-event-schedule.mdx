## The Daily Reset and Clock Penalties

The foundational element of the **tomodachi life event schedule** is the daily reset, which occurs at **5:00 AM** every day. At this moment, the game refreshes its internal logic, determining which items will appear in shops and resetting the donation box at the fountain. 

It is vital to note that Tomodachi Life is extremely sensitive to manual time changes. If you attempt to "time travel" by changing your Nintendo system clock, the game will detect the discrepancy. This usually results in a lockout period of at least 24 to 48 hours where no daily events will update, shops will remain stagnant, and special items will not appear.

| Event Type | Reset Time | Frequency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Daily Specials** | 5:00 AM | Daily |
| **Fountain Donations** | 5:00 AM | Daily |
| **Marketplace Refresh** | Multiple (See below) | 3x Daily |
| **Weekly Renovations** | Friday/Saturday | Weekly |

> **Warning:** Avoid changing your system clock to skip ahead. Doing so freezes all timed progression and can prevent you from collecting rare seasonal items for several days.

## The Marketplace Timed Schedule

The Marketplace is perhaps the most time-sensitive location on your island. Unlike the standard shops, the Marketplace offers unique items that are often unavailable elsewhere. To collect everything, you must visit during three distinct windows throughout the day.

| Market Session | Start Time | End Time |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Morning Market** | 5:00 AM | 11:30 AM |
| **Afternoon Market** | 11:30 AM | 5:00 PM |
| **Night Market** | 5:00 PM | 5:00 AM |

The items sold here change with every session. If you are aiming for a 100% completion rate in your encyclopedia, checking the Morning Market is often the hardest hurdle for players, but it frequently contains regional food items or rare accessories that rarely appear in the standard Supermarket or Clothing Shop.

## Daily Shop Rotations and Inventory Management

Every morning at 5:00 AM, the Clothing Shop and the Supermarket update their "Daily Specials." These are a curated selection of items that are only available for 24 hours. Given that there are over 8,000 individual items (including color variations) in the game, missing a single day can mean waiting months—or even a year—for that specific item to return to the **tomodachi life event schedule**.

### The Clothing Shop
The Clothing Shop is the primary source of customization. Each day, a handful of new outfits are featured. It is highly recommended to purchase every new item, even if you don't currently have a Mii that fits the style. Building your collection is the only way to fill the encyclopedia.

### The Supermarket
The Supermarket features four daily specials. Food is critical for managing Mii happiness. Every Mii has a "Super All-Time Favorite" and a "Worst Ever" food. By purchasing all daily specials, you can systematically test different foods on your Miis to unlock their unique reaction animations and gain massive friendship boosts.

### The Renovation Center
Unlike the other shops, the Renovation Center operates on a weekly **tomodachi life event schedule**. A new interior theme is introduced once a week, typically resetting on Friday or Saturday. These rooms are often the most expensive items in the game, sometimes costing thousands of dollars. Since they only rotate once a week, you have plenty of time to save up your currency.

## Maximizing Daily Income: Fountain and Pawn Shop

To afford the expensive weekly renovations, you need a steady stream of income. The game provides two primary daily methods for this:

1.  **Fountain Donations:** Every day after 5:00 AM, visit the fountain. Your Miis will leave donations based on their current happiness levels and the total population of your island. To increase this amount, focus on solving Mii problems (represented by bubbles over their windows) and increasing their friendship levels.
2.  **The Pawn Shop:** This shop doesn't sell items to you; instead, you sell treasures to it. Treasures are primarily obtained through Mii dreams or as rewards for leveling up a Mii.

| Item Source | Best Time to Check | Reward Type |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fountain** | Morning (Post-5 AM) | Cash (Direct) |
| **Mii Dreams** | Night (Late Evening) | Treasures (Pawn Items) |
| **Level Ups** | Anytime | Treasures/Coin |

> **Tip:** Adding more Miis to your island directly correlates to higher daily donations at the fountain. A full island of 100 Miis provides a significant financial boost every morning.

## Nightly Activities: The Science of Mii Dreams

As the day ends, the **tomodachi life event schedule** shifts toward nightly activities. Once your Miis go to sleep, you will notice purple thought bubbles appearing over their heads in the apartment view. This indicates they are dreaming.

Entering a Mii's dream allows you to witness some of the game's most surreal content, but more importantly, it is a guaranteed way to earn treasures. You can generally view one dream per Mii per night. Once the dream sequence ends, the Mii will "give" you an item from the dream. These items can be sold at the Pawn Shop for varying amounts of money. Some rare treasures can fetch a high price, making the nightly "dream sweep" a vital part of your financial routine.

## Mii Needs and Friendship Cycles

Miis follow a semi-predictable hunger and social cycle. They are most likely to be hungry in the early morning immediately following the daily reset. As the day progresses, Miis may occasionally feed themselves, which reduces the friendship points you can earn by feeding them personally.

To optimize friendship growth, check your island in the morning and feed every Mii their favorite foods. This not only boosts their level but also increases the likelihood of them asking to interact with other Miis, which triggers the social events (confessions, friendships, and breakups) that drive the game's narrative.

## FAQ

**Q: What time does the Tomodachi Life event schedule reset each day?**

A: The official daily reset occurs at 5:00 AM. This is when shop specials refresh, fountain donations become available, and Mii hunger levels typically peak.

**Q: Why are my shops not updating even though it's a new day?**

A: This usually happens if the system clock on your Nintendo 3DS or hardware has been changed recently. The game penalizes "time travel" by freezing all timed events for 24-48 hours.

**Q: How do I get more items in the Morning Market?**

A: The Morning Market (5:00 AM – 11:30 AM) is fixed in its timing. To see different items, you must check back daily. The stock is randomized but influenced by your island's overall progress and regional settings.

**Q: When is the best time to collect treasures for the Pawn Shop?**

A: Nighttime is the best period for treasure hunting. Look for Miis with purple bubbles above their heads while they sleep to enter their dreams and receive treasure items upon exiting.
