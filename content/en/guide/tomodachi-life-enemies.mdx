<Section icon={Map} title="Triggers for Rivalry and Jealousy">

Why do Miis become **tomodachi life enemies**? Usually, it comes down to their internal personality matrices or external social pressures. The game’s engine calculates compatibility based on the personality charts (Easygoing, Outgoing, Independent, and Confident).

The most common trigger is **Jealousy**. As seen in social simulations, if Mii A is friends with Mii B, and Mii C attempts to "insert" themselves into that dynamic or shows romantic interest in Mii B, Mii A may react aggressively. This creates a rift that can ruin long-standing relationships.

| Trigger Factor | Description | Likelihood of Fight |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Personality Clash** | Opposing traits (e.g., Confident vs. Easygoing) | 25% |
| **Romantic Rejection** | One Mii rejects another's confession | 60% |
| **Love Triangle** | Two Miis liking the same person | 85% |
| **Interrupted Hangout** | A third Mii barging in on a duo | 40% |

<Callout type="tip" title="Island Management Tip">
To reduce the number of **tomodachi life enemies**, try to group Miis with similar personality types in neighboring apartments. While this doesn't guarantee peace, it reduces the frequency of "Personality Clash" triggers.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Target} title="How to Resolve Mii Fights">

When your Miis are literally "on fire" with rage, you must take action. The process of reconciliation is not always guaranteed to work, and the outcome often depends on the current relationship level between the two parties.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Aggressor">
    Look for the Mii with the fire icon. Tap their apartment to enter. They will usually be pacing back and forth, venting about the other Mii.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Listen to the Complaint">
    The Mii will explain why they are angry. They might say things like "I'll never forgive them!" or complain about the other Mii's behavior (e.g., "They walk like they're on a runway!").
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Wait for a Mediator">
    In major fights, a third Mii (a mutual friend) will often appear with a "thought" bubble. They will offer to help the two enemies patch things up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Apology Phase">
    The mediator brings both Miis together. One will attempt to apologize. You must watch the interaction play out; you cannot influence the dialogue here.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Reconciliation Outcome | Resulting Relationship Status | Sadness Bar Increase |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Full Forgiveness** | Restored to "Getting Along OK" | 0% |
| **Partial Forgiveness** | "Not Talking" status remains | 10% |
| **Total Refusal** | Relationship Deleted / Enemies | 50% - 100% |
| **Counter-Argument** | Conflict escalates further | 25% |

<Callout type="success" title="Success Story">
If a reconciliation goes well, both Miis will feel a massive sense of relief. This is the fastest way to turn **tomodachi life enemies** back into friends and clear any lingering sadness bars.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Long-Term Effects of Enmity">

Having **tomodachi life enemies** on your island isn't just a visual nuisance; it has tangible effects on the gameplay loop. Miis who are enemies will refuse to participate in certain group activities together and may even bad-mouth each other to you, the player.

**Key Consequences of Long-Term Rivalry:**
- **Decreased Happiness**: Miis involved in constant conflict gain less happiness from gifts and food.
- **Social Isolation**: Other Miis may start to avoid the "angry" Mii, leading to a lonelier island experience.
- **Divorce/Breakups**: If a married couple or dating pair has a major fight that isn't resolved, it almost always leads to a breakup.
